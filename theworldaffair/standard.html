TheWorldAffair — Editorial Standard

Discipline opérationnelle — en vigueur 18 mai 2026

Operating discipline for every edition. Issued 18 May 2026.

The gazette aspires to be a publication of record: independent, critical, deeply-sourced, one step ahead of mainstream media. This document codifies the ten disciplines that make that aspiration enforceable.

1. SOURCE DISCIPLINE

  • Every factual claim ships with at least one named source + URL inline.
  • Quantitative claims require two INDEPENDENT sources. Echo-citations (Fortune quoting Ramp; Reuters citing AP) DO NOT count as a second source.
  • Where only one source exists, the claim ships with a 🟡 Source unique badge.
  • Primary sources are preferred in this order: regulatory filings (SEC, AMF, OJEU, Federal Register) → peer-reviewed journals (NEJM, Nature, Science, bioRxiv preprints flagged as such) → primary data (Mauna Loa CO2, EIA STEO, AIS feeds, on-chain) → tier-1 newswires (Reuters, Bloomberg, FT, AP).
  • Press releases are NOT sources. They are claims by interested parties; cite the release URL only as evidence of the claim, not as confirmation of the fact.

2. INCENTIVE LAYER

Every récit closes with a Qui paie · Qui profite : micro-line (≤ 15 words) naming the financial, political, or reputational beneficiary. If unknown: write Qui paie · Qui profite : unclear — to track.

The gazette never assumes good faith without naming whose good faith it's assuming.

3. CONFIDENCE LABELS

Each récit ships with one of three badges, placed immediately after the title:

  • 🟢 Confirmé — primary source available, two-source rule met.
  • 🟡 À vérifier — one source, plausible, surfaced but hedged.
  • 🔴 Spéculation — analytical inference. Useful but explicitly labeled.

Default to the most conservative tier. Promote only on evidence.

4. INDICATEURS AVANCÉS

Every domain ships a 4-6 row table of leading metrics at the top:

IndicateurValeurΔ vs édition précédenteSource

These are proxies that beat press releases by weeks: hyperscaler capex, tanker AIS gaps, ClinicalTrials.gov registrations, CISA KEV adds, stablecoin float, MOVE index, port-call volumes, Mauna Loa CO2 — domain-specific. The full proxy playbook is in NEXUS/OUTPUT/TheWorldAffair-AUDIT.md.

The gazette tracks indicators because the news cycle is downstream of the data.

5. RÉTROSPECTIVE (in Synthèse)

Every Synthèse opens with a 5-line ## Rétrospective section:

"In edition N°X we said A. New information: B. We were [right / partially right / wrong] because C."

If nothing was overturned, say so explicitly. Silence on corrections = complicity with one's own past errors.

6. CE QUI RESTE OPAQUE

Every major récit closes with 🤔 Ce qui reste opaque : (1-2 lines) naming what's still unresolved and what would change the reading. This is intellectual honesty made structural.

7. CONSÉQUENCE OPÉRABLE

Replace generic "ce qui change" with Conséquence opérable (7-30j) : — 1-2 sentences naming a concrete observable downstream effect to watch in the next 7-30 days. If a reader cannot act on the prediction, the prediction is decoration.

8. CROSS-CHECK PROTOCOL

The top-3 stories of each edition (identified during synthesis) get an independent verification pass: a different agent, different sources, different angle, before publish. The cross-check produces either confirmation (proceed), refinement (rewrite), or kill (drop the récit).

9. NO PARTY LINE

Apply the same skepticism to: Trump administration, EU Commission, big tech, climate organizations, Wall Street consensus, Chinese state press, Russian state press, and the gazette's own prior framing. The Propagande & Narratifs domain may critique any previous edition of TheWorldAffair.

The gazette earns its independence by directing skepticism at every interest — including the interests the editorial team finds sympathetic.

10. THE ARNAUD TEST

Before publish, each récit must pass:

"Would Arnaud learn something he could not get from Le Monde + FT + Reuters + a good podcast?"

If no: kill the récit. Volume is not value. The gazette publishes important news. It does not publish noise.

DOMAIN COVERAGE

The gazette covers ~22 consolidated domains per edition. Volume targets:

  • 3-5 récits per domain (no more — pick THE 3 to 5)
  • 1200-1600 words per domain (with the discipline above embedded)
  • Each édition is readable in 25-35 minutes
