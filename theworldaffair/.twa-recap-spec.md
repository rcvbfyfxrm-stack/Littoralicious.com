# NEXUS A — THE WORLD AFFAIR — WEEKLY RECAP (SAMEDI / LIVE)
> Committed spec, loaded by the Saturday GitHub Action. It produces ONE weekly recap edition — **the most interesting of the whole week, across ALL 9 beats.** It SHARES every standard with the daily spec: read `theworldaffair/.twa-magazine-spec.md` too for the LOI ÉDITORIALE, the source tiers, the **T3-floor rule**, the styled components, the **`.twa-verdict` typographic stamp (NEVER emoji)**, and the French literary voice. This spec only changes the SHAPE (a week, not a day) and the SELECTION (best-of, not rotation).

## STEP 0 — DATE + WEEK WINDOW
DATE = today (Samedi), `date +%F`. The "week" you recap = the **last 7 days** of daily editions. Read `theworldaffair/editions.json`; note every `kind:"daily"` edition from the past ~7 days — those are the week you're recapping.

## STEP 1 — HARVEST THE WEEK (read what ran, then refresh)
1. Read the week's daily edition files that already shipped — `theworldaffair/<date>.html` and their `synthese.html` + feature files under `theworldaffair/<date>/` — to see exactly what was covered across the 9 beats and with what verdicts.
2. Then WebSearch to confirm how the week's biggest developments RESOLVED (a story that was « À vérifier » Monday may be « Confirmé » or dead by Saturday) and to catch anything the dailies missed.
3. Across ALL 9 beats (Mer & Navigation · Table & Terroir · Marchés & Capital · Silicium & Intelligence · Pouvoir & Frontières · Corps & Longévité · Ciel & Énergie · Culture & Création · Bonnes Nouvelles) select the **7–10 stories that MATTERED MOST this week** — importance × surprise × durability (would it still matter in a month?). Tier every claim; apply the T3-floor rule; a verdict may be UPGRADED or DOWNGRADED vs. the daily as the week's evidence firmed up.

## STEP 2 — METADATA
- NUMBER = (highest `number` in editions.json) + 1. WEEKDAY = today in French, capitalized. VERSION = « v1.1 — hebdo ». FILE = "$DATE.html". kind = "weekly".
- CLUSTER = a 2–3 word theme tying the whole week (evocative, and NOT among the last 7 clusters in editions.json — force novelty).
- DOMAIN_COLOR: La Semaine #8b3a3a · L'Histoire de la Semaine = the lead story's beat colour (see the daily spec's colour list).

## STEP 3 — WRITE THE RECAP (Read .article-template.html, fill, Write)
**La Semaine** → `theworldaffair/$DATE/la-semaine.html` — DOMAIN=« La Semaine », color #8b3a3a, TITLE=« [Cluster] — La semaine du [dates] ». {{BODY}}:
- Open on the SINGLE most important thing that happened all week — payoff first, incarnated.
- « Les Sept Jours » — walk the week's 7–10 biggest stories grouped by **through-line** (not by beat), each a short punchy récit with a `.twa-verdict` stamp + inline `<a href>` sources; note where the week CHANGED a daily verdict.
- « Chiffres de la Semaine » — a table of the week's MOVES (S&P, Nasdaq, BTC, ETH, VIX, Oil, Gold, EUR/USD, 10Y + any beat-specific number), each with Δ over the week and a source.
- « Le Fil de la Semaine » — the one thread that connects the week (2–3 paragraphs).
- « À surveiller la semaine prochaine » — 3–5 forward items with the trigger/date to watch.
- « L'Angle Mort de la Semaine » — the one genuinely important story the herd under-covered ALL week (breadth + independence).

**L'Histoire de la Semaine** (optional but preferred) → `theworldaffair/$DATE/histoire-de-la-semaine.html` — the single biggest story of the week, gone deep, DOMAIN = its beat, with the correct beat-class component (Le Tape / Carte des Preuves / Carte Cui-Bono / Le Fait du Jour) and the full récit fields.

Min ~25 paragraphs in La Semaine. Tight, literary, payoff-first French. Cite tier-1 sources.

## STEP 4 — FRONT PAGE (Read .front-template.html, fill, Write to theworldaffair/$DATE.html)
Replace {{WEEKDAY}}, {{NUMBER}}, {{CLUSTER}}, {{VERSION}}, {{FILE}}=$DATE.html, {{DATE}}=$DATE, {{SUBTITLE}}=« Le récap de la semaine », plus:
- {{SIXTY_SECONDS}} = exactly 3 `<li>` — the three biggest stories of the WEEK, payoff-first, each with a source link.
- {{CARDS}} = 2–3 `<a class="hub-card">`: 1) La Semaine → `$DATE/la-semaine.html` (overline « Récap Hebdomadaire », color #8b3a3a); 2) L'Histoire de la Semaine → `$DATE/histoire-de-la-semaine.html` (overline = its beat, beat colour); optionally 3) a card linking back to `archive.html` (« Toute la semaine »).

## STEP 5 — MANIFEST
Read `theworldaffair/editions.json`. **FIRST remove any existing entry whose `date` equals $DATE** (a re-run REPLACES, never stacks). THEN prepend: `{"date":"$DATE","weekday":"WEEKDAY","number":NUMBER,"kind":"weekly","cluster":"CLUSTER","title":"Récap de la Semaine","summary":"<one-line teaser>","file":"$DATE.html"}`. Valid JSON, keep every OTHER entry. Do NOT touch index.html / archive.html.

## STEP 6 — DO NOT PUBLISH YOURSELF
Leave all files on disk. Do **NOT** run STEP 2g of the daily spec and do **NOT** run any `git` command — the GitHub Action commits, pushes and deploys for you. Your job ends when the files are written and valid.
