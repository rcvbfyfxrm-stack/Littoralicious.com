Battered Fish

Vodka and rice flour suppress gluten. Beer + double-leavening lifts the lace. The dredge welds it to the fish. Cold batter into 185°C oil, the fillet held submerged for 5 seconds before release.

Yield: 4 fillets Active: 35 min Total: 1 hr 15 Oil: 185°C (365°F) Internal: 60–63°C

Method

  1. Brine fillets in 1 L water + 30g salt — exactly 15 min. Lift, dry triple paper-towel, hold uncovered on a rack in fridge. 3% brine seasons through and tightens surface proteins. Wet fish = batter slides off. — Nosrat 2017
  2. Heat oil to 185°C in a 4 L pot, 8 cm depth. Clipped probe thermometer. < 170°C: batter absorbs oil before set — greasy. > 195°C: crust browns before fish cooks. — Kenji 2014
  3. Whisk dredge in one bowl. Whisk batter dries in a second. Beer in fridge, vodka in freezer.
  4. At 185°C: pour cold beer + freezer vodka into batter dries. Whisk 10–15 strokes only — rice-grain lumps fine. Use immediately. Gluten forms the moment flour meets water; CO&sub2; leaves the moment beer is open. Rested 10 min = half-deflated. — Heston 2006
  5. Dredge fillet in seasoned flour (thin coat). Batter, lift by tail, drip 2–3 sec.
  6. Lower head-first; hold tail submerged 5–8 sec before releasing. Sets the crust uniformly, prevents sticking. Drop and release = sinks, sticks, tears. The single most important hand-skill. — Kenji 2014
  7. 2 fillets max per batch. Fry 5–7 min for a 2.5–3 cm fillet, turn at 3 min. Done at uniform deep gold + audibly crackles + fish reads 60–63°C. More than two crashes oil below 165°C; greasy fish.
  8. Drain 5 sec above pot, transfer straight to wire rack. Never paper towels. Never foil. Paper traps steam, softens underside in 90 sec. Wire rack lets steam escape from all sides.
  9. Salt with Maldon within 5 sec of leaving oil, from 20 cm height. Salt bonds to hot oily crust in the first 5 sec only.
  10. Hold in 90°C oven on rack, uncovered, between batches (max 10 min). Don’t stack. Serve on warm plates with lemon, tartare, malt vinegar.
Done: Uniform deep gold · crust audibly crackles when tapped with the spider · internal 60–63°C (140–145°F). No double-fry: that’s for chips, not fish.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • Rice bran oil swap — cleaner crust, higher smoke point
  • ½ tsp turmeric in dredge — subtle golden hue
  • 25% beef dripping in oil — northern English depth
  • Black pepper in batter — 1g coarse in dries
Tier 2 — Worth It
  • Homemade tartare — mayo + cornichons + capers + shallot
  • Mushy peas — marrowfat soaked overnight, mint, butter
  • Triple-cooked chips — Heston’s potato
  • Lager-pickled shallots — chip-shop tradition, 24 hr ahead
Tier 3 — Restaurant
  • Goujons / fish bites — 3 cm strips, 90 sec fry
  • Tempura direction — sparkling water, 70% rice flour
  • Sourdough discard in batter — tang + tighter crust
  • Charred lemon wedges — halved, dry-skillet 3 min

Troubleshoot

Soggy crustOil cold / overcrowded / drained on paper. 2 fillets max, rack only.
Greasy batterOil < 170°C / overmixed. Hold 185°C, 10–15 strokes.
Batter slid offWet fish / no dredge / drop from height. Pat dry, dredge, lower at surface.
Pale crustOil under temp / no baking soda. 185°C non-negotiable.
Burnt outside, raw insideOil > 200°C / fillet too thick. Drop to 185°C; butterfly > 3 cm.
Crisp then soggyHeld in foil / stacked. Wire rack, 90°C oven, uncovered.
Tastes flatSkipped brine / salted at plate. Brine 15 min, salt at rack within 5 sec.
Tough, dry fishInternal > 65°C. Probe to 60–63°C max.

Charter Prep & Storage

Pre-portion dry batter175g vacuum bags = 4 fillets each. 1 month pantry.
At serviceTip bag in bowl, add cold beer + freezer vodka, fry. 90 sec mise.
Vodka200ml lives in freezer permanently. Sorbets too.
Frozen fishPollock IQF: thaw 24 hr fridge, brine as fresh.
Tartare sauce5 days fridge sealed jar. Improves overnight.
Never fry underwayHot oil + motion = fire. In port only. Oil disposal: shore.
Don’t freeze cookedSoggy inside, tough outside. Fry to order. No exceptions.
ScalingAlways small batter batches — CO&sub2; dies past 16 fillets.
