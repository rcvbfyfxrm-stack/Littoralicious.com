Sticky Toffee Pudding

Dates bloomed with soda for a silky crumb. Dark muscovado + treacle for caramel that’s deep, not flat. A two-stage sauce finished off-heat with whisky, poured hot so the sponge drinks it dark.

Yield: 10–12 (20×20 cm) Active: 15 min Total: 1 hr 15 Oven: 170°C (340°F) Done: 94°C internal
Where it was bornEngland by name, contested by fact — oldest claim the Gait Inn, Millington, Yorkshire (1907); the famous line runs through Lancashire from two Canadian wartime airmen.
Eaten, authenticallyHot, the sponge skewered and drowned in toffee sauce, with cold clotted cream — a country-house dining-room pudding made famous at Sharrow Bay in the 1970s.
How it’s used nowA pub and restaurant fixture worldwide; the same date batter becomes sticky ginger & walnut pudding (below), and the sauce stands alone over ice cream.

The ratio — flour = 100% Flour 100 · Sugar 100 · Butter 57 · Egg 57 · Dates 125. A near 1:1:1 pound-cake frame carried by its own weight again in soft date — that date load holds the sauce.

Kit: 20×20 cm (8″) square pan or 8 ramekins · heavy small saucepan · scale · instant-read thermometer · whisk · skewer

Method

  1. Bloom the dates. Stir 6g soda into 280g boiling water until clear, pour over 220g pitted dates, stand 15 min. Blend smooth; cool to ≤40°C before the batter. Alkaline water (pH ~8) dissolves pectin so the purée goes silky + pre-reacts the dates’ acid; hot purée melts the butter emulsion. — McGee 2004
  2. Cream the sugars. Oven 170°C (340°F); butter + line the pan. Cream 100g butter, 175g muscovado, 25g treacle 3–4 min until pale. A little grain is fine. Dark muscovado = up to ~15% molasses vs light brown’s ~3.5%: deeper colour, smoke, dark fruit + moist crumb. — Parks, BraveTart 2017
  3. Eggs, then flour + dates. Beat in 100g egg one at a time with vanilla. Whisk flour, baking powder, salt; fold in half → all dates → rest. Stop at 80% — streaks fine. Over-mixing builds gluten + air → a tall springy cake that sheds sauce. — Beranbaum, Cake Bible
  4. Bake + rest. 30–35 min to 94°C (200°F) centre — matte, cracked edges, skewer tacky not wet. Rest 10 min in the pan; do not unmould. At 94°C the starch is set but porous and warm — the state that drinks hot sauce. — McGee 2004
Whisky Caramel — two stages, off-heat finish
  1. a. Melt 100g butter + 150g muscovado low until glossy, 3–4 min. No graininess.
  2. b. Off heat: whisk in 200g cream + vanilla. Back on low, simmer 90 sec to coat a spoon — never a hard boil, which splits it.
  3. c. Off heat: whisk in 30g whisky + 3g flaky salt. Whisky’s esters boil off above 78°C — add late, hold 70–80°C. — Modernist Cuisine 2011
  4. d. Skewer + drown: 30+ holes in the warm sponge, pour ⅓, let it vanish 60 sec, then plate + drench ~40g a portion. Reserve the rest for the table.
Done: Sponge 94°C (200°F) internal, matte + cracked, skewer tacky. Sauce coats a spoon, glossy, unbroken.
Serve: hot, soaked dark, with cold clotted cream / crème fraîche / ice cream. Pour the sauce at 70–80°C — never cold, it gels on top.
One batter, two puddings — sticky ginger & walnut The same date sponge, the Lancashire variation: add 4g ground ginger to the dry mix, 30–40g chopped stem ginger + 60g toasted walnuts to the batter. Same pan, same 94°C, same whisky caramel (or a maple butterscotch). The sauce also stands alone — 5 days fridge, gentle reheat over ice cream.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • Silan glaze — swap treacle for 25g date molasses
  • Pedro Ximénez — 40g PX for the whisky; fig + raisin
  • Espresso — 1g instant into the sauce if skipping booze
Tier 2 — Worth It (+10 min)
  • Brown-butter sponge — brown 130g, weigh 100g
  • Burnt-honey brake — 30g honey to mahogany into the sauce
  • Miso caramel — 15g white miso whisked in; deeper caramel
Tier 3 — Restaurant
  • Smoked dates — cold-smoke 20 min over oak before soaking
  • Smoked clotted cream — 15 min hay/applewood, covered
  • Bone-marrow crust — 20g rendered marrow + salt on the warm top

Troubleshoot

Metallic tasteSoda not dissolved. Stir into water until clear first.
Dense, gummyDate purée too hot. Cool to ≤40°C — it melts the butter.
Sunk middleUnderbaked. Bake to a true 94°C, not on colour.
Sauce splitBoiled too hard. Off heat, splash cold cream, whisk hard.
Sauce sits on topPoured cold / crumb cold. Pour at 70–80°C on warm sponge.
Tastes flatLight brown, not muscovado. Use muscovado, or +25g molasses.

Charter Prep & Storage

The ruleStore pudding + sauce APART, always. Together = split fat, mushy cake.
Baked pudding2 mo frozen. Reheat 120°C / 12 min, lightly pre-soaked.
Toffee sauce2 wk fridge / 3 mo freezer. Skim fat, whisk back in 30-sec bursts.
Date base5 d fridge in a jar. Double the soak for next week.
Raw ramekins1 mo frozen. Bake from frozen +10 min @ 165°C.
NeverStore the two together, or pour the sauce cold.
