Sourdough at Sea — The Wild-Yeast Loaf

A living culture, a long cold proof, a hot covered bake. The wild loaf made reliable on a moving galley — and the acidity that flavours it also makes it keep.

Yield: 2 loaves (10–12) Active: 40 min Total: 2–3 days Oven: 250→230°C (covered) Done: 96–99°C internal
Where it was bornThe oldest leaven there is — wild yeast in flour and water, the only way to raise bread before industrial yeast in the 1860s. Egyptian loaves >3,000 yrs ago; the name from Klondike prospectors who guarded the crock through winter.
Eaten, authenticallyThe everyday country loaf of every grain culture — torn warm with oil or butter; San Francisco gave it a famous tang and a bacterium named for the city.
How it’s used nowThe craft-bakery boule — and the chef’s living culture, carried boat to boat down the dock. Crumb for brunch, toast, bruschetta; stale, it makes panzanella and ribollita.

The ratio — flour = 100% Water 75% · Levain 20% · Salt 2%. Four numbers; everything above is one batch of them, scaled for two crew loaves. Learn them and you never need the recipe again.

Kit: Dutch oven or combi/Rational · 2 bannetons (or towel-lined bowls) · lame / razor · scale · bench scraper · mixing tub · probe thermometer

Method

  1. Build the levain. Night before: 20g starter + 100g flour + 100g water. 4–8 h until doubled and a spoonful floats. A purpose-built levain gives a known, vigorous population at a known time; the float test reads enough gas to lift the loaf. — Robertson; Modernist Bread
  2. Autolyse. Mix 800g bread + 100g wholemeal flour with 600g water to a shaggy mass — no levain, no salt. Rest 45 min. Flour hydrates and gluten knits with no work; salt and acid now would tighten it too early. — McGee; López-Alt
  3. Add levain, then salt. Pinch in the ~200g levain; dissolve 20g salt in the reserved 50g water and work through. Dissolved salt spreads evenly and reins the yeast to a pace the long proof can ride. — Forkish
  4. Bulk + 4 folds. 24–26°C, ~4–5 h. Four stretch-and-folds in the first 2 h. Stop at ~40% risen, domed, jiggly. Folds build aligned gluten that traps big bubbles — the open crumb. Judge by rise, not the clock; a warm galley halves it. — Robertson
  5. Pre-shape & shape. Cut in two, pre-shape, bench 25 min, shape taut boules, seam-up in rice-floured bannetons. The rest stops tearing; a tight skin holds the loaf through proof and bake. Rice flour has no gluten, so it won’t glue. — Forkish
  6. Cold proof 12–36 h. Bag and into the fridge at 4°C. Bake straight from cold, no warm-up. Yeast crawls while bacteria build acid — deeper flavour, blistered crust, clean scoring; and it uncouples bake from proof so you fire to order. — Modernist Bread
Bake: preheat Dutch oven/combi 250°C. Turn out cold, score one slash, lid on 20 min; lid off, 230°C, 20–25 min to deep mahogany / 96–99°C internal. Cool ≥2 h before cutting — the crumb sets as it cools.
Keep it alive at sea — the catch is the water. Chlorine, chloramine and stripped water-maker product stall a culture — feed bottled spring water, or stand tap water uncovered overnight. Warm galley (26–30°C) ferments fast (feed more, use cooler water); a cold fridge holds it dormant a week+ between feeds.
Carry it anywhere — dehydrate Smear ripe starter paper-thin on parchment, dry below 40°C (dehydrator, low oven, shaded deck), flake and vacuum-seal. Survives months and flights in a pocket; revive over two or three feeds. Insurance against a crossing crew who bins your jar.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • Stiff levain (60% water) — milder, sweeter
  • Longer cold proof — more tang, to 36 h
  • Tin + foil lid — when shaping or sea won’t cooperate
Tier 2 — Worth It
  • Country loaf — 20–30% wholemeal/rye, +water
  • Seeded — 150g soaked seeds after the salt
  • 80–85% hydration — wilder, holier crumb
Tier 3 — Service
  • Olive & rosemary laminated in the folds
  • Bâtard — more crust, easy sandwiches
  • Discard → crackers, crumpets, blinis

Troubleshoot

Dense, tightLevain not ripe / under-proofed → float test; bulk to ~40% domed
Flat, spreadsOver-proofed / weak shape → catch bulk earlier, shape taut, proof <36 h
Gummy centrePulled pale → bake to 96–99°C; cool 2 h before cutting
Too sourLevain old / fridge warm → use younger, stiffer levain; verify 4°C
Pale, no springPot/oven cool → true 250°C, deeper score, lid on 20 min
Starter sluggishChlorine / water-maker water → bottled water, warm corner
