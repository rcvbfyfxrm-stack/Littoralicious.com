Roast Lamb Shoulder — Overnight

Twelve hours at 110°C drives connective tissue past 90°C internal — collagen melts, fat renders, fibres pull apart under a fork. Dry brine is half the crackle. The blast at the end, never the start. Shoulder only — not leg (leg above 60°C is dry, no recovery; see leg variant in Tier 2).

Yield: 6–8 Active: 30 min Total: 10–13 hr Oven: 110°C → blast 220°C Internal: 90°C

Kit: roasting tray with rack · leave-in probe thermometer (alarm 90°C) · oven thermometer (most ovens drift ±15°C at low temp) · mortar/pestle · fat separator · two forks.

Method

  1. Dry brine 12–24 hr ahead. Pat dry. Score fat cap in 2 cm crosshatch (through fat, not muscle). Massage salt + pepper all over. Set on rack, uncovered, in fridge. Salt 24 hr ahead = seasoned through + dry surface (better crackle). The 30-min window is the trap. Either 24 hr or right before. — Nosrat 2017
  2. Make the rub. Pound garlic + anchovy + rosemary + thyme + zest to paste. Stir in oil, mustard, pepper. Anchovy: glutamate + inosinate, umami synergy ~8×. Undetectable as fish; reads as deeper roast. — Ottolenghi 2014
  3. Set the bed and rack. Bed in tray, ~2 cm liquid. Pull shoulder, rub paste all over (into score lines). Rack into tray, fat-cap up. Probe thickest muscle, away from bone. Alarm 90°C.
  4. Overnight: 110°C (230°F). Centre rack. Close oven door. Do not open it. 8–12 hr until probe reads 90°C. Below 95°C internal: collagen never converts. Above 100°C oven: fibres squeeze water faster than collagen converts. 110°C / 90°C is the corridor. — McGee 2004
  5. The blast: 220°C, 15–20 min. Move shoulder to a clean tray (so the bed doesn’t scorch). Watch closely — line between mahogany and burned is two minutes. Sprinkle Maldon as it leaves the oven. Maillard at the END, not the start. Browning at start wastes the colour — volatiles evaporate over 10 hours. Browning at end keeps them on the meat.
  6. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented in foil.
  7. Build the jus. Strain bed liquid through fine mesh. De-fat (jar the fat — exceptional for roast potatoes). Reduce by ⅓ to ½. Season last.
  8. Pull at the table with two forks. Bone slides out. Pile on warm plates with jus, parsley, mint, lemon wedge.
Done: Probe reads 90°C internal in thickest muscle, away from bone. Past 90°C every additional hour pulls texture from “tender” to “spoon-soft.” Bone slides out cleanly, fork meets no resistance. Fat cap mahogany after the blast.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • Anchovy in rub — already in base. Don’t skip.
  • Save the rendered fat — lamb-fat roast potatoes (separate dinner)
  • Garlic confit on side — the bed’s garlic head, after 10 hr, is already confit
  • Pickled red onion — acid + crunch against jammy meat, 30 min
Tier 2 — Worth It
  • Reverse-sear leg — 80°C to 50°C internal, blast 240°C 8–12 min
  • 7-Hour Provençal — sealed cocotte with white beans, garlic
  • Greek kleftiko — parchment+foil package, lemon-oregano
  • Mechoui spice — ras-el-hanout + cumin + smoked paprika + smen
Tier 3 — Restaurant
  • Sous-vide 24 hr at 75°C — max collagen / min protein loss
  • Hay-baked — smouldering hay in covered Dutch oven
  • Pomegranate-molasses lacquer — last 10 min of blast
  • Charcoal-grilled crackle — finish over hot embers

Troubleshoot

Dry, fibrousWrong cut (leg) / past 95°C too long. Pull 90–93°C.
No crackleSurface wet / oven low. Dry-brine 24 hr; blast 220°C.
Burnt cap, raw insideBlast at start. Always blast AFTER 90°C internal.
Jus weak / wateryBed light / not reduced. Reduce by half; cold butter end.
Probe 90°C, meat firmProbe near bone. Reposition into thickest muscle.
Tastes flatSalt only at surface. Dry-brine 24 hr next time.
