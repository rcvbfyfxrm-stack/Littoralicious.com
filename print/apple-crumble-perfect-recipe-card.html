Apple Crumble

Two apple varieties, 60:40 — one holds, one melts. A 71°C stovetop pre-cook that firms the fruit before it bakes. A brown-butter oat topping scattered loose and salted on top.

Yield: 10–12 (30×20 cm) Active: 25 min Total: 1 hr 30 Oven: 200°C (400°F) Done: 93–96°C filling
Where it was bornBritain, 1940s — a wartime thrift dish. With butter and sugar rationed, the pastry pie gave way to flour rubbed into fat over stewed fruit. Named for what it does.
Eaten, authenticallyA hot pudding, brought to the table in its dish and spooned warm — with cold crème anglaise (custard), cream, or ice cream against the syrupy fruit.
How it’s used nowA British autumn staple in every register; the oat-topped version is, by the old rule, an American crisp. Apple is the classic; rhubarb and blackberry-apple are the cousins.

The ratio — topping by weight Flour 3 · Oats 2 · Sugar 2.8 · Butter 2.8. Lean by design — flour and oats outweigh the fat, so it bakes to loose rubble, not a greasy sheet. Filling runs ~17 : 1 fruit to sugar — the apples carry the sweetness.

Kit: 30×20 cm (or 23 cm round) dish, 5 cm deep · wide sauté pan · light pan for brown butter · scale · instant-read probe · sheet tray under, for bubble-over.

Method

  1. Prep the fruit. Oven to 200°C (400°F); butter the dish. Peel, core + cut both apples to 2 cm chunks. Toss with lemon first, then sugar, cornflour, cinnamon, 2.5g salt. Firm high-acid apples hold; soft low-acid ones melt. 60:40 = chunk and bind in one spoonful. — McGee pp. 361–364
  2. Pre-cook to 71°C. Melt 36g butter, foaming; add apples + liquid. Cook 8–10 min, stirring, until the melts apple softens, the firm one stays cubed, liquid reduces to glossy syrup — centre 71°C (160°F). Pectin methylesterase (50–80°C) demethylates pectin; freed carboxyls cross-link with cell-wall calcium → firm, heat-stable fruit that bakes tender, not mush. — Van Buggenhout / McGee pp. 285–286
  3. Spread + cool. Tip into the dish, level, cool to warm, not hot, while you make the crumble. Topping laid on hot fruit steams from below + never crisps. Cool first.
  4. Brown 168g butter. Light pan, medium, swirl. Foam, subside, golden solids, toasted-hazelnut smell — 6–8 min. Pull immediately (carryover browns on). Cool to warm. Past ~120°C the milk solids run Maillard → hundreds of nutty, caramel aromatics. The biggest flavour for 30 sec of effort. — McGee pp. 32–38
  5. Build the crumble. Whisk flour, oats, both sugars, salt, cinnamon. Pour warm brown butter over (every bit); fork to pea-to-walnut pebbles, not sand. Range = texture. Old-fashioned oats absorb fat without dissolving + add chewy bite. Instant melts back to flat streusel. — Figoni 2010
Done: Deep golden, mahogany edges; thick syrup bubbling through the cracks (not just a simmer at the rim); filling 93–96°C (200–205°F). Bake 200°C on a tray, rotate once, 35–45 min.
Serve: Top warm fruit with the crumble loose — do not pack (air gaps crisp it). Scatter flaky salt before baking. Rest 10 min (cornflour sets at ~75°C), serve warm with custard, crème fraîche, or ice cream. Never microwave; never serve straight from the oven.
One filling, two puddings — apple charlotte Double the pre-cooked filling, reduce half a little thicker. Line a deep tin with butter-soaked white bread, crust-side out; pack the apple in tight; cap with more buttered bread. Bake 200°C, 30–35 min to deep gold, rest, then turn out — a crisp golden case round a dense apple centre. The older English pudding the crumble half-replaced.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • Calvados — 15ml into the pre-cook, cook off
  • Toasted oats — dry-toast 3 min before mixing
  • Orange zest — with the lemon, in the fruit
Tier 2 — Worth It
  • Blackberry + apple — 150g berries into cooled filling
  • Spiced brown butter — cardamom, clove, star anise; strain
  • Pecans / hazelnuts — 60g toasted, folded into crumble
Tier 3 — Restaurant
  • Brown-butter ice cream — the right scoop
  • Miso caramel — 100g sugar, 50g butter, 20g miso, 75g cream
  • Apple-chip garnish — 1mm slice, dried crisp

Troubleshoot

Watery bottomSkipped/under-reduced pre-cook. Reduce to syrup, not water.
All mushOne variety or over-pre-cooked. 60:40; pull at 71°C.
Centres rawChunks too big / no pre-cook. 2 cm max; true 200°C.
Pale, soggy topOven cool / packed / filling hot. 200°C, loose, cool fruit.
Greasy toppingButter too hot / over-mixed. Warm butter; fork to pebbles.
Filling poursServed too hot. Rest 10 min — cornflour sets at ~75°C.
