Yorkshire Pudding

A steam balloon held up by a fragile gluten-and-egg shell. Bread flour for the cage. ~115% hydration for the steam. Smoking-hot dripping for the seal. A sealed oven door for the climb.

Yield: 12 muffin / 4 popover Active: 20 min Total: 35 min Oven: 230°C (450°F) Fat: smoking

The Four Non-Negotiables
1. Bread flour——
2. ~115% hydration——
3. Smoking fat——
4. Sealed oven, no peek——

Kit: carbon-steel popover or 12-cup muffin tin · oven thermometer (most ovens read 15–20°C low) · 1 L jug · whisk · sieve · digital scale.

Method

  1. Sieve flour + salt into a bowl. Make a well. Crack eggs into the well; whisk hard into the flour from the centre out — paste, then thick batter, then smooth. Salt with the flour, never on rested batter — it won’t dissolve and you’ll taste grains in the crust.
  2. Stream in cold milk while whisking until the batter has the consistency of single cream. Pass through a sieve into a 1 L jug. ~115% hydration is the steam fuel. Drier batter = no climb. Volume cups under-pack flour; weigh the four numbers above. — Kenji 2014
  3. Rest the batter — or don’t. 30 min room temp / overnight in fridge / not at all. Heston rests; Kenji’s side-by-side found no difference. Both right. The four non-negotiables matter more than rest. — Heston 2006 / Kenji 2014
  4. Heat oven to 230°C (450°F) fan-off, or 220°C fan. Place empty tin on the centre rack. Verify with thermometer.
  5. Add fat after 5 min — 1 tsp dripping per muffin cup, or 2½ tsp per popover. Heat 8–10 min until visibly smoking. Not shimmering — smoking. Smoke = 200°C+, hot enough to set the batter base in 1–2 sec and trap steam inside. RSC 2008: “very hot fat” is binding. — RSC 2008
  6. Pull tin onto open oven door. Pour cold batter fast — fill each cup ½ to ⅔. Will hiss and spit. Correct.
  7. Slide tin in. Close the door. Do not open it again. Each door-open at 230°C drops oven 25–40°C; recovery 90 sec. Unset puddings collapse and never recover. The #1 home failure. — Cloake 2012
  8. Bake 230°C × 15 min, undisturbed. Climb happens at minutes 6–12 — watch through the glass.
  9. Drop to 190°C without opening the door. Continue 5–10 min until deep amber and the rims dry. Total 20–25 min muffin / 25–30 min popover. Drop dries the crust without scorching. First 15 min makes the climb; final 5–10 min sets the colour.
  10. Pull tin out. Lift puddings free. 5–7 cm tall (muffin) or 8–12 cm (popover) with a deep crater.
Done: Deep amber, rims dry, hollow crater. Eat within 5 minutes — deflates from peak in 10. Time the bake to land as the beef rests.
If you must hold: 90°C oven, rack, uncovered, max 8 min. Never stack. Never foil.

Elevation

Tier 1 — No Extra Time
  • True beef dripping — render from roast trim, freeze in ice-cube trays
  • +1 yolk — richer crust, more tender centre
  • Maldon at the rim — sprinkle as they leave the tin
  • Carbon-steel popover — 8–12 cm climb vs 5–7 cm
Tier 2 — Worth It
  • 12-hr fridge rest — Heston’s signature; pull 30 min before pour
  • Toad-in-the-hole — brown sausages in dripping, pour batter around
  • Used dripping from the roast tin — peak savoury layer
  • Onion gravy fill — pour into pudding crater at service
  • Horseradish cream — crème fraîche + grated fresh root
Tier 3 — Restaurant
  • Suet-rendered dripping — pre-1950s tradition, deepest savoury
  • Plate-sized filled Yorkshire — pudding as the bowl, beef + greens + gravy
  • Compound-fat tin — 60% dripping + 30% bone-marrow + 10% shallot oil
  • Yorkshire wrap — flat tray, slice warm, fill with thin beef + horseradish

Troubleshoot

No rise / flatFat not hot. Smoke 8–10 min. Verify oven 230°C.
Climbed then collapsedDoor opened / pulled too early. Bake full 25 min.
Rim crisp, base soggyInsufficient fat / batter overfilled. 2–3 mm fat depth, fill ½–⅔.
Greasy crustFat below smoke point. Wait for visible smoke.
Dense, eggyPlain flour / under-egged. Bread flour, weigh 220g eggs.
Lopsided climbFat uneven / oven hot-spot. Spoon evenly.
Pale crustOven too low. Verify thermometer; bake to amber.
Stuck to tinTin not hot at fat-in. Heat tin first, then add fat.
