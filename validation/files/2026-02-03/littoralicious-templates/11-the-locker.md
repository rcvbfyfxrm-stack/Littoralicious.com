### Brand Research Checklist

Before recommending a brand, verify:
- [ ] Production method (traditional vs. industrial)
- [ ] Origin authenticity (is it actually from where it claims?)
- [ ] Ingredient list (hidden additives, flavor enhancers?)
- [ ] Professional reputation (used in respected restaurants?)
- [ ] Availability (can it actually be sourced reliably?)
- [ ] Shelf stability (appropriate for yacht storage?)
- [ ] Price-to-quality ratio at each tier

### Quality Markers to Research

- **Soy Sauce:** Naturally brewed (6+ months) vs. hydrolyzed (days)
- **Olive Oil:** Harvest date, acidity level, certification (DOP/IGP)
- **Fish Sauce:** First press vs. subsequent, protein content, aging time
- **Vinegar:** Barrel-aged vs. quick-process, mother culture
- **Miso:** Aging duration, rice-to-soy ratio, regional style
- **Dried Pasta:** Bronze-die extrusion, drying time, semolina quality
- **Spices:** Whole vs. pre-ground, origin specificity, harvest date

### Voice Reminders

- Specific: "Aged 18 months in cedar barrels" not "traditionally made"
- No marketing language: Not "artisanal," "curated," or "elevated"
- Honest about limitations: "Harder to source outside [region]" or "Quality varies by batch"
- Assume competence: Don't explain what miso is; explain which miso and why
