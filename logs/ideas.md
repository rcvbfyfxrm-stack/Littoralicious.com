## Articles — The Evidence
- **Decoding what Michelin really means by "cooking over fire"** — "Le feu est imprecis, imprevisible, vivant. Il est le contraire exact du bain-marie a 56,3 degres. Quand Michelin valorise le feu, il valorise le risque, la main du cuisinier, le moment irreproductible." Fire as signature tool, not rustic accessory.
- **Mushrooms as the new protein centre** — shiitakes replacing steak, king oyster as escalope, pleurotes whole-roasted at centre of plate. Not vegan activism — gustatory pragmatism. A good mushroom has the umami of aged meat and the texture of a noble product.
- **Tea as cooking ingredient** — not as beverage. Hojicha in sauces, matcha in marinades, lapsang souchong in cooking jus. Aromatic complexity that classical fonds can't reproduce. Chefs in 2026 use tea with the same rigour they used wine twenty years ago.
- **Garlic: history and reputation** — why this reputation in our collective unconscious? Science, mythology, cultural weight.
- **Physical taste of food** — aliment physique, how physical form affects taste perception
- **Weed and cooking** — cannabis as culinary ingredient, the science, the legal landscape, the flavour profiles
- **Chocolate and heart health** — what the evidence actually says
- **Matcha: beyond the hype** — the real science of catechins, L-theanine, and culinary applications
- **Simon Rogan / Simon Mills teaching approach** — what does the science say about chef pedagogy?
