Salt-Cured Anchovies: Seasonal Guide, Compound Profile, and Sourcing

Spring anchovies are weeks away. Engraulis encrasicolus — the European anchovy that built a Mediterranean industry — peaks between March and July. The colatura drawn from its salt-cured barrels peaks right now, two and three years into fermentation. Here is everything you need to know to source both at their best.

The Species

European anchovy, 12–18 cm, family Engraulidae. Approximately 30% of total Mediterranean fishery production — annual landings averaging nearly 400,000 tonnes. The fish that built an industry.

The Catch Window

For Colatura di Alici di Cetara DOP, the production specification mandates anchovies harvested between March 25 (Feast of the Annunciation) and July 22 (Feast of Mary Magdalene), from waters within 12 nautical miles off the coast of Salerno province. Spring-caught fish have superior fat composition. The Ortiz family in Cantabria buys exclusively from the spring season for the same reason.

Catch window: March 25 – July 22. Spring-caught anchovies have optimal fat composition for both salt-curing and colatura production. This is not tradition for tradition’s sake — it is biochemistry.

Compound Profile

Compound Per 100 g Raw Notes
Protein 17–26 g Varies by season; spring fish optimal
EPA + DHA (omega-3) 1.3–2.1 g combined DHA averaging 9.4% of total lipid, EPA 7.9%
Free glutamic acid (colatura) 5,600–7,300 mg/L Increases substantially between 24 and 48 months of barrel aging
Histamine (properly produced) 44 mg/kg at 1 month, declining EU limit: 400 mg/kg. Extended aging decreases histamine in properly handled product

Extended aging decreases histamine in properly handled product. This is counterintuitive but well-documented. For guests with histamine sensitivity, longer-aged DOP colatura is actually the safer product.

DOP Status

Colatura di Alici di Cetara received Denominazione di Origine Protetta in October 2020 — the first Italian DOP product in the fishery sector. Specifications require: anchovies from Salerno coastal waters, traditional terzigni (chestnut wood barrels), minimum 12 months aging, bottled in transparent glass (50–1,000 mL).

Sourcing

When transiting the Amalfi Coast, buy direct from Cetara — Delfino Battista and Nettuno are the principal artisanal producers. European yacht provisioners (Gourmet Deliveries in Cannes/Monaco/Antibes, Just Provisions across the Med, Riviera Gourmet on the Italian coast) carry DOP-certified bottles.

For salt-packed whole anchovies: Ortiz (Cantabria, Spain) and Recca (Sicily) are benchmark brands available at most provisioning stops. Stock both colatura and salt-packed fillets — they serve different purposes and neither substitutes for the other.
