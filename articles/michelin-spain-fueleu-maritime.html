Michelin Spain 2026 Expands and FuelEU Maritime Enters First Compliance Cycle

Two developments that cross the gangway this week: Spain’s Michelin constellation grows to 307 starred restaurants, and FuelEU Maritime enters its first full compliance cycle with deadlines running through June 2026. One shapes where your guests want to eat ashore. The other shapes how the vessel gets there.

Michelin Guide Spain 2026 Expands

The Michelin Guide Spain & Andorra 2026 added 30 new starred restaurants, bringing the total to 307 Michelin-starred establishments across Spain and Andorra. The guide continues to recognise Spain’s position as one of Europe’s deepest restaurant cultures.

Separately, Michelin Guide Great Britain & Ireland 2026 held its ceremony on February 9 in Dublin — the first time Ireland hosted the event. New stars and Green Stars were announced. Asia’s 50 Best Restaurants will be announced March 25 in Hong Kong, another first for the city.

In the US, the James Beard Foundation announced 2026 semifinalists on January 21. Final nominees will be revealed March 31; the ceremony takes place June 15 at the Lyric Opera of Chicago.

For the galley: Spain now has 307 Michelin-starred restaurants. If guests ask for dining recommendations during a Western Med charter, the density of starred options in Spain — particularly along the Basque coast, Catalonia, and the Balearics — is unmatched.

FuelEU Maritime: First Full Compliance Cycle

FuelEU Maritime enters its first full compliance cycle in 2026, imposing a declining greenhouse-gas intensity standard on energy used on board ships over 5,000 GT calling at EU ports. This runs alongside the EU ETS (now at 100% emissions coverage — see Issue 2).

Deadline Requirement
January 31, 2026 Vessel emission reports submitted
March 31, 2026 Third-party verification complete
April 30, 2026 Verified compliance logged
June 30, 2026 Penalties issued for non-compliance
Post-June 2026 FuelEU Documents of Compliance issued

Your move: For superyachts meeting scope tests (over 5,000 GT, commercial certification), 2026 is the first cycle where fuel documentation becomes a commercial lever. Confirm compliance timelines with your management company. The Mediterranean SOx Emission Control Area (0.10% fuel sulfur limit, in effect since May 2025) adds a separate compliance layer for all vessels operating in the Med.
