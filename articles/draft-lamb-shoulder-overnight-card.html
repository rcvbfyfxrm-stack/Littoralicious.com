Roast Lamb Shoulder — Overnight Galley Card

Yield: 6-8 · Active: 30 min · Total: 10-13 hr · Oven: 110°C → blast 220°C · Internal target: 90°C

Cut matters: This card is for shoulder only. Leg uses a different recipe (reverse-sear, 80°C oven to 52°C medium-rare). Do not apply overnight to a leg — it ruins it.

Ingredients

Lamb (Group A)

  • 1 bone-in lamb shoulder, fat cap intact, scored — 2.0-2.4 kg (Welsh / Pyrenean / NZ grass-fed)
  • Dry brine: 25 g coarse sea salt + 5 g cracked black pepper

Rub (Group B) — pound to a paste

  • 30 g garlic (8-10 cloves) · 20 g anchovy (4 fillets) · 10 g rosemary chopped · 5 g thyme chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon · 30 g olive oil · 15 g Dijon mustard · 3 g cracked pepper

Bed (Group C) — under the rack, builds the jus

  • 400 g onions halved, cut-side down · 300 g carrots in batons · 200 g celery halved
  • 1 garlic head halved · 4 bay · 3 rosemary sprigs · 5 g peppercorns
  • 250 ml dry white wine · 500 ml lamb or chicken stock

Service (Group D)

  • Maldon flaky sea salt · lemon wedges · parsley + mint chopped

Internal Temperature Targets

   SHOULDER (this recipe)         LEG (different recipe)
   ─────────────────────         ──────────────────────
   90°C  pulled, jammy, fork-tender    52°C  medium rare (carries to 56°C)
   95°C  spoon-soft, fall-apart        60°C+ overcooked, dry. NO recovery.
   < 88°C  still tough — keep cooking

Probe in the thickest muscle, away from bone (bone reads cold).

Method

  1. Dry brine 12-24 hr ahead. Pat shoulder dry. Score fat cap in 2 cm crosshatch (through fat, not muscle). Rub salt + pepper all over. Set on rack, uncovered, in fridge 12-24 hr. The dry surface is half the crackle.¹
  2. Make the rub. Pound garlic + anchovy + rosemary + thyme + lemon zest to a paste. Stir in oil, mustard, extra pepper.
  3. Set the bed. Onions cut-side down + carrots + celery + garlic head + bay + rosemary + peppercorns in roasting tray. Pour over wine + stock — about 2 cm liquid, no more.
  4. Rub and rack. Pull shoulder from fridge, rub paste all over (into score lines). Set rack in tray, shoulder fat-cap up on the rack. Insert probe into thickest muscle. Alarm at 90°C.
  5. Overnight: 110°C (230°F). Centre rack. Close oven door. Do not open it. 8-12 hr until probe reads 90°C.²
  6. The blast: 220°C (425°F), 15-20 min. Move shoulder to a clean tray (so the bed doesn't scorch). Watch closely — line between mahogany and burned is two minutes. Sprinkle Maldon as it leaves the oven.³
  7. Rest 30 minutes loosely tented in foil.
  8. Build the jus. Strain bed liquid through fine mesh. De-fat (jar the fat — exceptional for roast potatoes). Reduce by 1/3 to 1/2 in a small saucepan. Season last.
  9. Pull at the table with two forks. Bone slides out. Pile on warm plates with jus, parsley, mint, lemon wedge.

Critical Points

¹ Dry brine 24 hr — the cheapest upgrade. Surface dries (better crackle), salt diffuses through the muscle (seasoned through, not just on edges). The 30-minute window is the trap — either 24 hr or right before. Never in between.

² 110°C / 90°C internal is the corridor. Below 95°C internal the collagen never converts; above 100°C oven the fibres squeeze out water faster than collagen converts. The 8-12 hr at 110°C oven, 90-95°C internal window is non-negotiable for shoulder. Past 90°C internal, every additional hour pulls texture from "tender" to "spoon-soft."

³ Final blast at the END, not the start. Maillard browning at the start wastes the colour — volatile aromatics evaporate over the next 10 hours. Browning at the end keeps them on the meat. Salt at the end of the blast lands on hot fat and bonds.

Charter Protocol: Cook-Ahead, Reheat-to-Order

You are not running an overnight oven during charter dinner service. Pre-cook the shoulder, refrigerate, reheat at service.

  1. Day before charter: salt + rub the shoulder, hold uncovered in fridge.
  2. Night before service: slow-roast 8-10 hr at 110°C. Pull at 90°C internal. Refrigerate whole on its rack, on the bed-tray.
  3. Day of service, 90 min before: pull from cold storage. 130°C oven, 45 min, until warm through.
  4. Final blast: 220°C, 15 min — restores the crackle the fridge softened.
  5. Rest 30 min, pull, serve.

Yacht-Galley Notes

  • Roast in port. A 2 L tray of hot stock and rendered fat in a moving oven is a hazard. Calm passages only, never rough seas.
  • Save the rendered fat. Strain through cheesecloth, jar. 1 month fridge / 6 months frozen. Roast potatoes in lamb fat is a separate dinner.
  • Probe placement. Thickest muscle, parallel to bone, not touching it. Bone reads cold and lies.
  • Underpowered oven (won't go below 130°C): cook 6-7 hr instead of 10-12, watch the probe. Final blast unchanged.
  • Boneless rolled shoulder (no bone-in available): add 1 hour. The bone was helping.
  • Leftovers: pulled lamb in jus, 3 days fridge / 2 months frozen. Best second-day meal in the kitchen — shepherd's pie, ragù, flatbread wraps, bao.

Leg Variant — Different Recipe, Different Method

For leg of lamb, reverse-sear: salt 24 hr ahead. 80°C (175°F) oven to 48°C internal (about 2-2.5 hr for 2.5-3 kg). Rest 20 min open (carries to 50-52°C). Then 240°C (465°F) blast, 8-12 min to 54-56°C medium rare. Rest 15 min. Slice against the grain.

Never apply overnight to a leg. Leg above 60°C is dry. No recovery.
