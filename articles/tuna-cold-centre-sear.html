Searing Tuna: The Cold-Centre Technique

Start with partially frozen tuna. Sear at maximum heat for 30–45 seconds per side. The frozen core acts as a thermal buffer, keeping the interior raw while the exterior develops a Maillard crust. No resting. Slice immediately. Here is the technique, the science behind it, and the three ways it fails.

The Five Steps

  1. Temper from freezer. Remove saku block from freezer 20–30 minutes before service. Target: firm exterior, still frozen at core (internal temp −5 to 0°C).
  2. Dry and season. Pat bone-dry with paper towels. Season with flaky salt only. No oil on the fish.
  3. Heat the pan. Heat a carbon steel or cast iron pan to smoking — 260°C+ surface temperature. Add a thin film of high-smoke-point oil (grapeseed, rice bran).
  4. Sear. 30–45 seconds per side. Four sides if the block is square-cut. Do not touch it while searing — movement prevents crust formation.
  5. Slice immediately. Remove. Slice with a sharp yanagiba or slicing knife into 8mm slices. The cross-section should show a 2–3mm seared ring around a raw, jewel-red centre.

Why It Works: The Maillard Gradient

The Maillard reaction requires surface temperatures above 140°C. At 260°C+ pan temperature, the amino acids and reducing sugars on the fish surface react in under 30 seconds, creating pyrazines (nutty, roasted) and furanones (caramelised). Meanwhile, the frozen core absorbs the inward-moving heat, preventing the interior from exceeding 10°C.

The temperature gradient is steep: 260°C at the surface, −5°C at the centre, 3cm apart. That gradient is the technique.

When It Fails

Factor What Happens Adjust By
Fish too thawed Centre overcooks; grey band widens Start from semi-frozen; 20 min max at room temp
Pan not hot enough Steaming instead of searing; no crust Wait until oil shimmers and first wisp of smoke
Fish wet Steam barrier prevents Maillard Triple-pat with paper towels; salt draws surface moisture

You know it worked when: The seared face is dark bronze, almost charred. The slice reveals a razor-thin brown ring around raw crimson. The centre is cool to the tongue. The guests stop talking.
