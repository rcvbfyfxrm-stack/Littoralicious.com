Yorkshire Pudding — Galley Card

Yield: 12 muffin / 4 popover · Active: 20 min · Total: 35 min · Oven: 230°C · Fat: smoking

Ingredients

ComponentWeightNotes
Strong bread flour140 gBread, not plain — gluten holds the climb
Whole eggs (large)4 (220 g shelled)Weigh — recipe wants 220 g
Whole milk220 g~115% hydration; thin like single cream
Fine sea salt4 gWith the flour, never on the rested batter
Beef dripping60-80 g5-7 g per muffin cup / 12-15 g per popover cup

Equipment: carbon-steel popover or 12-cup muffin tin · oven thermometer · 1 L jug · whisk · sieve.

Oven & Fat Targets

   COLD       PREHEAT TIN   ADD FAT     SMOKING FAT    BAKE
  < 200°C  ── 230°C/5 min ── (open) ── 230°C/8-10 min ── pour
  no climb   tin must be hot          smoke = GO

Verify oven with a thermometer — most ovens read 15-20°C low. Fat must visibly smoke before the batter pours.

Method

  1. Sieve flour and salt into a bowl. Make a well.¹
  2. Whisk eggs into the flour from the centre outward — paste → thick batter → smooth. Slowly stream in cold milk while whisking until the batter has the consistency of single cream.¹
  3. Pass through a sieve into a 1 L jug. Catches lumps.
  4. Rest the batter — 30 min at room temp, or overnight in the fridge, or not at all. Heston rests; Kenji does not; the four non-negotiables matter more.²
  5. Heat oven to 230°C (450°F) fan-off, or 220°C fan. Place the empty tin on the centre rack for 5 minutes.
  6. Add fat — 1 tsp dripping per muffin cup, or 2½ tsp per popover cup. Return tin to oven 8-10 minutes until visibly smoking. Not shimmering — smoking.³
  7. Pull tin onto the open oven door. Pour cold batter fast — fill each cup ½ to ⅔. The batter will hiss and spit. Correct.
  8. Slide tin in. Close the door. Do not open it again until step 10.⁴
  9. Bake 230°C × 15 minutes, undisturbed. Watch through the glass — climb happens at minutes 6-12.
  10. Drop oven to 190°C without opening the door. Continue 5-10 minutes until deep amber and the rims dry. Total 20-25 min muffin / 25-30 min popover.⁵
  11. Pull tin out. Lift puddings free. Should be 5-7 cm tall (muffin) or 8-12 cm (popover) with a deep crater.
  12. Plate immediately. Eat within 5 minutes. Yorkshire deflates from peak in 10 minutes. Time the bake to finish as the beef rests.

Critical Points

¹ Salt with the flour, never on rested batter. Won't dissolve evenly; you'll taste grains. Whisk eggs into the flour first — fully homogenised before milk thins it.

² Rest debate. Heston rests overnight (full hydration, relaxed gluten). Kenji's side-by-side found no measurable difference. Both right. Rest if you have time; skip if you don't. The four non-negotiables — bread flour, high hydration, smoking fat, sealed oven — matter more than rest.

³ Smoking fat is non-negotiable. Smoke means 200°C+, hot enough to set the batter base in 1-2 seconds and trap steam inside. Cold fat lets batter pool, soak, and never climb. RSC 2008: "very hot fat" is a binding requirement.

⁴ No peeking. Each door-open at 230°C drops oven temperature 25-40°C; recovery takes 90 seconds. In that window, unset puddings collapse and never recover. This kills more home Yorkshires than any other cause.

⁵ The temperature drop dries the crust. First 15 min at 230°C makes the climb; final 5-10 min at 190°C dries the rim deep amber without scorching.

Yacht-Galley Notes

  • Bake to order. Yorkshire pudding deflates in 10 minutes. Time it to land as the beef rests. Never hold longer than 8 min in a 90°C oven, uncovered, on a rack.
  • Beef dripping ice-cubes — render trim from each roast, freeze in ice-cube trays (~5 g cubes). One cube per muffin cup at service. Holds 6 months at -18°C.
  • Pre-mixed batter — mix the morning before, refrigerate sealed. Pull 30 min before pour to take chill off.
  • Rough seas: do not attempt — pourable hot fat in motion is a fire risk. Postpone.
  • No fan oven: drop to 200°C at minute 15 instead of 190°C. Same climb, slightly less rim colour.
  • Do not freeze raw batter — eggs and milk separate, hydration drops.

Sources: Heston Blumenthal (overnight rest, bread flour, smoking dripping), J. Kenji López-Alt (no-rest test, hydration), Felicity Cloake (Guardian comparative test), Harold McGee (steam and gluten mechanics), Royal Society of Chemistry (2008 four-inch rule). Full citations in the deep recipe.
