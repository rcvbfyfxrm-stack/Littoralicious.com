Provisioning Miami & Fort Lauderdale: The Complete Playbook

The provisioning capital of the Americas — if you know which Costco to drive to. Zero import tax, multiple wholesale clubs that grant free membership with boat registration, suppliers covering every quality tier from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu — and provisioning agents marking the same Halpern’s beef up 18% because the chef did not know to walk past them. This is the playbook to skip the markup, time the seasons, and load everything you can carry before the Caribbean leg starts.

The Brief
Why this port is different — and what to set up on day one.
5 min ▸

Port type: major hub. Agent markup runs 15–18%. On a USD 15,000 provision, self-sourcing saves USD 2,200–2,700. The wholesale infrastructure is so good here that using an agent is paying someone to do what you can do better yourself — unless you genuinely have zero time.

If your season starts here or you transit through, load everything you can carry. Prices only go up from here.

Miami has zero import tax, multiple wholesale clubs that grant free membership with boat registration, and suppliers covering every quality tier from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu. Two memberships secured on day one — Costco Business Center + Restaurant Depot — cover 60–70% of any provision at wholesale prices.

If you are sole chef, separate your orders by category across different days. One day for proteins. Another for dry store. Another for fresh produce + specialty. Each run has a different rhythm and different suppliers. Trying to do everything in one day means you rush the decisions that matter most.

The Bottom Line
If you read nothing else.
2 min ▸

Miami is the provisioning capital of the Americas. Zero import tax, wholesale infrastructure everywhere, every cuisine and quality tier covered from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu. The single best move is getting the Costco Business Center and Restaurant Depot memberships on day one — between them, they cover 60–70% of any provision at wholesale prices. Add a wholesale account with Halpern’s and you cut out the agent markup on proteins.

Before you leave, load Caputo flour, Asian pantry staples (168 Market), and frozen vac-packed proteins (Wild Fork / Snake River) for the next three ports.

Avoid the late-October Boat Show, the December Art Basel week, and any named-storm watch June–November. Those three windows alone account for most of the operational pain in this port.
