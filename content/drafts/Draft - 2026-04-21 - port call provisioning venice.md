# Provisioning Venice: The One-Page Playbook

*Docked at Stazione Marittima. A dawn market three bridges away that has fed this city for a thousand years. A lagoon orchard eight kilometres north-east that grows vegetables found nowhere else on earth. A fishing fleet an hour south that lands more of the Adriatic's catch than any other port in Italy. Drogheria Mascari, Casa del Parmigiano, Bisol, Quintarelli, Perenzin — all of them answer the phone. The only mistake you can make in Venice is to treat it like a provisioning problem.*

**Port type:** World-class artisan larder with an unusual last-mile. Charter hub with direct truck and water access at Marittima.
**Chandler/agent markup range:** 18-28% — the top end buys access (Rialto stalls, Chioggia auction, Sant'Erasmo farm direct); the bottom end buys commodity. Pay for the access.
**The single most important thing:** No van. No Metro run. No Mestre shortcut at the expense of the product. Venice's supply chain is porter + water-cargo + supplier boats, and every serious chef in this city uses it. The Marittima gate accepts trucks from the mainland *and* cargo water-taxis from the lagoon — both deliveries land at the same berth. Use both.

---

## THE THREE-TIER DECISION

Pick the tier that matches the time you actually have. Do not mix tiers — the access breaks down.

| Tier | Time window | Best for | What you get |
|------|-------------|----------|--------------|
| **Tier 1 — The Named Agent** | Under 24 hours | Late arrivals, guest preferences changed, menu rewrite | Rialto-grade produce and fish, Mascari dry store, Casa del Parmigiano cheese — all sourced that morning, trucked to Marittima. 22-28% markup. |
| **Tier 2 — The Chef's Circuit** | 2-3 days | The chef does Rialto and Mascari in person; porters and water-cargo do the moving | Direct relationships with stall owners, first pick, the produce nobody puts out |
| **Tier 3 — The Producer's Circuit** | 4+ days | Flagship charter, menu designed around what Venice uniquely grows, catches, ages | Sant'Erasmo by farm boat, Chioggia auction with a buyer's agent, estate wine from Valdobbiadene and Valpolicella, affineur cheese |

# TIER 1 — 24 HOURS: THE NAMED AGENT

The difference between a Venice agent and a Mediterranean commodity agent is everything. You are not paying 22-28% for delivery. You are paying for a buyer who walks Rialto Pescheria at 07:30 with your list, who has a line open to the Chioggia cooperative for the fish that doesn't reach Rialto, and who has a trade account at Casa del Parmigiano and Drogheria Mascari. Refuse anyone who substitutes a wholesale-produce box for Rialto. That substitution is the entire job.

## The 24-hour sequence

1. **T-24h:** Send the list by WhatsApp + PDF. Italian specifications where they matter: *Caputo 00, Mulino Caputo Cuoco, Acquerello rice, Parmigiano Reggiano Vacche Rosse 30 mesi, Prosciutto di Parma DOP 24 mesi, olio extravergine DOP Veneto Valpolicella o Garda orientale.* Saying "good parmesan" buys you generic grana padano.
2. **T-18h:** Confirm line-by-line price and substitutions in writing. Refuse *al prezzo di mercato* without a number. Require the agent to name the Rialto stall and the Chioggia cooperative on the invoice — if they can't name them, they aren't buying them.
3. **T-12h:** Confirm Marittima gate access. The port authority requires vehicle registration and driver name in advance; the agent handles it, but confirm.
4. **T-0:** Check every crate dockside before signing. Fish cold-chain is summer's silent killer — touch every box for temperature. Refuse anything below spec without argument.

> **The move:** *Specify "acquisto diretto a Rialto, banco [VERIFY stall name via WhatsApp intel]" on the list.* Naming the stall forces the sourcing. A commodity agent will push back; that's the point.

**Red flags:** Cannot name the Rialto stall or Chioggia cooperative. Refuses line-item invoicing. Cash-only above EUR 500. Substitutes "market produce" for Rialto without explanation. Walk.

# TIER 2 — 2-3 DAYS: THE CHEF'S CIRCUIT

You have two or three days. You go to the market. The porter and the water-taxi move the weight. This is the Venice every serious restaurant chef here lives in — Rialto at dawn, Drogheria Mascari mid-morning, Casa del Parmigiano on the walk back, a caviste in the afternoon, everything consolidated at a quayside drop and run by cargo water-taxi to Marittima. No van. No bridge to Mestre. No compromise on quality.

**The last-mile stack (what replaces the van):**
- **Porter service (facchini):** A professional porter meets you at the market with a two-wheel trolley. Contact: [VERIFY — the Rialto porters' cooperative is the standard; ask the stall you buy from for a number]. EUR 30-60 per run. Non-negotiable if you are buying more than two crates.
- **Cargo water-taxi (taxi acqueo merci):** Quayside at Rialto (Riva del Vin) or San Basilio to Marittima. EUR 80-150 per run depending on volume and operator. Book 60-90 min ahead. Operators: Consorzio Motoscafi Venezia, Venezia Taxi Motoscafi. Refrigerated units are rare — for fish and protein, use insulated crates with ice and keep the transfer under 30 min.
- **Commodity dry store (if needed at all):** Do not make the Mestre trip yourself. Order Metro Cash & Carry Mestre case-lot items online for delivery to Marittima (48-72h, minimum [VERIFY EUR 300-500]). This is the one task that belongs on a van — somebody else's van.

## Per-stop detail

### Rialto Pescheria

**Address:** Campo de la Pescaria, Sestiere di San Polo, 30125.
**Hours:** Tuesday-Saturday, 07:30-12:00. **Closed Sunday and Monday — fish.** Non-negotiable. If your charter starts Monday, plan for Saturday fish held on ice or buy from Chioggia direct (Tier 3) for Sunday delivery.
**What to ask for:** branzino and orata *di laguna* (wild, tag matters), moeche in season (late April-May, briefly October), seppie with ink sacs intact, canoce (mantis shrimp) live, cannocchie, sole, anguilla in autumn, day-boat tuna only if the stall will name the boat.
**Skip:** anything sold *decongelato* as fresh. Ask directly — *"fresco o decongelato?"* — and expect a straight answer.
**Payment:** cash preferred, card accepted at most stalls.
**Relationship:** Buy from the same stall three visits running. By the fourth, they show you the boxes they keep behind the counter.

> **The move:** Walk the entire length of the Pescheria before you buy anything. Prices and quality vary stall-to-stall within the same hour. The best stall this week is not the best stall next week.

### Rialto Erberia

**Address:** Same campo as the Pescheria. **Hours:** Monday-Saturday 07:30-13:00. (Produce is open Monday; fish is not.)
**What to ask for:** castraure di Sant'Erasmo (late April-May only, unique to the lagoon), bruscandoli (wild hop shoots, spring), radicchio rosso di Treviso tardivo IGP (December-February, peak), asparagi bianchi di Bassano DOP (spring), zucca mantovana, pomodorini del Cavallino, figs in summer from the northern lagoon.
**Skip:** imported off-season produce sold as local. Ask the grower's name; if they don't have one, keep walking.
**Payment:** cash at most stalls, card at larger ones.

> **The move:** For Sant'Erasmo castraure, ask which *brocca* — the terminal bud, the centre, or the lateral shoots. Each has a different use. A stall that can answer this is a stall that actually sources Sant'Erasmo.

# TIER 3 — TIME-RICH: THE PRODUCER'S CIRCUIT

Four days or more. The guest list justifies the effort. Veneto's producers are among the best in Italy, and every one of them named below will deliver to Marittima directly — by truck, by refrigerated van, by their own boat, or by the agent's boat. This is the Venice that makes a season of charter unforgettable.

## FISHMONGER / PESCHERIA

**Go-to:** **Rialto Pescheria** (see Tier 2). Tuesday-Saturday only; closed Sunday and Monday.

**Delivery:** No — Rialto is in-person only. For delivered fish, go to Chioggia direct below.

> **The move:** Build one stall relationship across the season. The fishmonger who recognises you on the third visit sells you the moeche that never hit the counter and the *go* (lagoon goby) that the restaurants book before dawn.

# THE BOTTOM LINE

Venice rewards the chef who refuses to compromise and punishes the one who takes the van to Mestre. Tier 1 is the named agent who buys at Rialto, Chioggia, Mascari, and Casa del Parmigiano on your behalf. Tier 2 is the dawn walk you do yourself — Rialto, Erberia, Mascari, Casa del Parmigiano, macelleria, panificio — with a porter and a cargo water-taxi moving the weight to Marittima. Tier 3 layers in Sant'Erasmo by farm boat, Chioggia via the cooperative, Bisol and Quintarelli via trade account, Perenzin for cheese. Stock up on Parmigiano, DOP olive oil, Prosecco, San Marzano DOP, Mascari spices, and long-life Venetian bread before you leave — the Croatian and Greek Adriatic is competent but not Italian. Verify every contact before committing: Venice's serious suppliers are small and private, and the names rotate through the chef grapevine, not through Google.
