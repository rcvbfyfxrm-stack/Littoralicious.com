# PORT CALL — Template

**Voice:** The chef who has provisioned this port three times and is handing you the shortcut. No options paralysis — one or two picks per category, tested, with the move that saves you time or money. Direct, supplier-specific, price-conscious.

**Rhythm:** Local reality → Three tiers by time available (24h / 2-3 days / time-rich) → Freezer-ready → Local gems → Bottom line.

**Word Count:** 2,000-3,500 · **Read Time:** 8-14 min

**Design principle:** A chef should be able to scan this on a phone, pick their tier based on time available, and move. Each tier is self-contained — the 24-hour chef never has to read the time-rich section. One or two suppliers per slot per tier. No decision fatigue.

**Companion file:** Every Port Call article has a companion file: `[city]-suppliers-extended.md` in the same directory. The article is the shortcut. The companion file is the encyclopedia.

## THE THREE-TIER DECISION

Pick the tier that matches the time you actually have. Do not mix tiers — the economics and logistics break down.

| Tier | Time window | Best for | Typical markup / loss |
|------|-------------|----------|----------------------|
| **Tier 1 — Agent Run** | Under 24 hours dock-to-charter | Fast turnarounds, late arrivals, guest preferences change | 15-22% over self-source |
| **Tier 2 — Efficient Run** | 2-3 days | Standard turnaround, one chef, minimal help | 3-7% over best price, saves a full day of driving |
| **Tier 3 — Artisan Map** | 4+ days, or provisioning for a long charter | Premium guest list, flagship menu, quality-first | -10 to +5% vs Tier 2, better quality end-to-end |

## The 24-hour sequence

1. **T-24h:** Send the full list by WhatsApp + PDF. Specify brands where brand matters, specs where it matters (size, grade, vintage, cut). Do not say "good steak" — say "Black Angus striploin, 2.5-3kg per piece, dry-aged 28+ days."
2. **T-18h:** Confirm price and substitutions in writing. Get a quote line-item, not a lump sum.
3. **T-12h:** Confirm delivery window and dock/gate access.
4. **T-0 (delivery):** Check every crate against the list on the dock, before signing. Refuse anything below spec.

## TEMPLATE NOTES (for authors — remove before publication)

### What makes a good Port Call article

1. **Three tiers, clean split.** Each tier is self-contained. A chef on a 24-hour turnaround never needs to read the artisan map. A chef with a week should not be bored by the agent section.
2. **Quality/price math is explicit.** Do not just list the agent — state the markup, state what quality grade you get, state whether it's worth it by category.
3. **"The move" is the value.** Anyone can list a shop name. The tip that saves 2 hours or EUR 500 is what earns the bookmark.
4. **Per-entry in Tier 3, split delivery vs go-to.** If a supplier delivers, the detail block is non-negotiable: minimum order, lead time, delivery days, window, contact method, packaging, invoicing. A chef planning a 4-day provision should be able to build the order schedule from the article alone.
5. **Provision forward.** Every article tells the reader what to buy here that disappears at the next port.
6. **Freezer-ready is a priority.** Vac-pack, portioning, blast-chill — identify and highlight. Applies across all three tiers.
7. **Source everything.** Every recommendation comes from working yacht chefs, not from Google.

### Tier decision checklist

Tier 1 — Agent Run:
- [ ] Named primary agent with contact
- [ ] Named backup agent
- [ ] Markup stated numerically by category
- [ ] Quality-vs-price table present
- [ ] Red flags listed

Tier 2 — Efficient Run:
- [ ] Total stops, total driving time stated
- [ ] Route ordered by time, not category
- [ ] Online vs in-person chosen per slot (not both)
- [ ] What this tier leaves out is honest

Tier 3 — Artisan Map:
- [ ] Fishmonger, butcher, fisherman-direct, épicerie, ethnic, baker, caviste, fromager, primeur — all covered or marked "not available"
- [ ] Each entry has both go-to AND delivery lines (delivery "No" is acceptable, omission is not)
- [ ] Delivery entries include: minimum, lead time, days, window, contact, packaging, invoicing

### Freezer-ready checklist

For every meat/fish/seafood supplier:

- [ ] Vacuum-pack? (free or charged?)
- [ ] Portion on request? (whole fillet, individual, both?)
- [ ] Lead time for portioned/packed orders?
- [ ] Blast chiller? (will they blast-chill your order?)
- [ ] Pre-frozen vac-packed products? (already blast-chilled at source)
- [ ] Minimum order for vac-pack/portioning service?

### Smart tips to look for

- Online ordering / click-and-collect / apps
- Wholesale card logistics (Metro, Costco — documents needed)
- Marina / dock delivery (who does, who does not, who has truck access)
- WhatsApp ordering (butchers, fishmongers, specialty)
- Cross-border or mainland-vs-island provisioning (Mestre not Venice, La Linea not Gibraltar, Nice not Monaco)
- Duty-free advantages / import tax traps / bonded vessel handling
- Market timing (arrival times, auction times, online cutoffs)
- Language — what to ask for in the local tongue
- What to buy HERE that disappears at the next port
- Freezer-ready intel (vac-pack, portioning, blast-chill)
