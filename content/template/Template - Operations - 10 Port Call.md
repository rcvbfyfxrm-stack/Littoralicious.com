# PORT CALL — Template

**Voice:** The chef who has provisioned this port three times and is handing you the shortcut. No options paralysis — one or two picks per category, tested, with the move that saves you time or money. Direct, supplier-specific, price-conscious.

**Rhythm:** Local reality → Provision map (with detail) → Freezer-ready → Agent → Local gems → Bottom line.

**Word Count:** 1,800-3,000 · **Read Time:** 7-12 min

**Design principle:** A chef should be able to scan this on a phone, place orders, and plan a provisioning run in under 30 minutes. One or two suppliers per category. No decision fatigue. No scrolling past the same information twice.

**Companion file:** Every Port Call article has a companion file: `[city]-suppliers-extended.md` in the same directory. The article is the shortcut. The companion file is the encyclopedia.

## TEMPLATE NOTES (for authors — remove before publication)

### What makes a good Port Call article

1. **No decision fatigue.** One or two picks per category. The chef does not need five options — they need the right one.
2. **Online + in-person for every category.** Some chefs plan ahead. Some arrive and need to move fast. Cover both.
3. **The move is the value.** Anyone can list a shop name. The tip that saves 2 hours or EUR 500 is what earns the bookmark.
4. **The agent section is honest.** State the markup, state the math, let the chef decide.
5. **Provision forward.** Every article tells the reader what to buy here that disappears at the next port.
6. **Freezer-ready is a priority.** Vac-pack, portioning, blast-chill — identify and highlight. This saves galley time and preserves quality.
7. **Source everything.** Every recommendation comes from working yacht chefs, not from Google.

### Freezer-ready checklist

For every meat/fish/seafood supplier:

- [ ] Vacuum-pack? (free or charged?)
- [ ] Portion on request? (whole fillet, individual, both?)
- [ ] Lead time for portioned/packed orders?
- [ ] Blast chiller? (will they blast-chill your order?)
- [ ] Pre-frozen vac-packed products? (already blast-chilled at source)
- [ ] Minimum order for vac-pack/portioning service?

### Smart tips to look for

- Online ordering / click-and-collect / apps
- Wholesale card logistics (Metro, Costco — documents needed)
- Marina delivery (who does, who does not)
- WhatsApp ordering (butchers, fishmongers, specialty)
- Cross-border provisioning (La Linea not Gibraltar, Nice not Monaco)
- Duty-free advantages / import tax traps
- Timing (market arrival times, online order cutoffs)
- What to buy HERE that disappears at the next port
- Freezer-ready intel (vac-pack, portioning, blast-chill)

### Companion file template

Every Port Call article gets a companion file: `[city]-suppliers-extended.md`

This file contains:
- **Every** supplier recommendation (not just the top 1-2 per category)
- Full contact details, addresses, phone numbers, websites
- Extended notes, hours, seasonal variations
- Suppliers that did not make the article cut but are still useful
- Guest-level / premium tier suppliers
- Historical intel (closed, changed, declined)
- Reader/community feedback and updates

The companion file is the living database. The article is the curated extract.
