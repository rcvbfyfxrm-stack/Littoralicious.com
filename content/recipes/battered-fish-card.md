# BATTERED FISH — GALLEY CARD

**Yield:** 4 fillets · **Active:** 35 min · **Total:** 1 hr 15 min · **Oil:** 185°C

---

## INGREDIENTS

| Component | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| White fish fillets, skinless, ≥ 2.5 cm | 720-800 g (4) | Cod / haddock / pollock / hake, MSC |
| Brine | 1 L water + 30 g salt | 3% — exactly 15 min |
| Dredge | 80 g plain flour + 3 g salt + 1 g pepper | The weld layer |
| Batter dries | 100 g plain + 50 g rice + 20 g cornflour + 4 g BP + 2 g BS + 3 g salt | Mix at last second |
| Batter wets | 200 ml fridge-cold lager + 50 ml freezer-cold vodka | (cold sparkling water 0-30 ml if thinning) |
| Oil | 2-2.5 L peanut or rice bran | 8 cm depth, 4 L pot |
| Service | Maldon · lemon · tartare · malt vinegar | At the rack, at the table |

---

## OIL TEMPERATURE TARGETS

```
   COLD     SET POINT     DROP        RECOVER     ABORT
  < 170°C   ─── 185°C ─── 160-170°C ── 180°C ──   > 200°C
  greasy    GO          (on drop)   keep frying   burn
```

Clip a probe thermometer to the side of the pot. Verify before every batch.

---

## METHOD

1. **Brine** fillets in 1 L water + 30 g salt for **exactly 15 minutes**. Lift, dry on triple paper towel, hold uncovered on a rack in the fridge.¹
2. **Heat oil** to **185°C** in a 4 L pot, 8 cm depth.
3. **Whisk dredge** in one bowl. **Whisk batter dries** in a second bowl. Beer in fridge, vodka in freezer until oil is at temp.²
4. At 185°C: pour cold beer + freezer vodka into batter dries. **Whisk 10-15 strokes only** — rice-grain lumps are fine. Use immediately.²
5. **Dredge** fillet in seasoned flour, shake off excess (thin coat).
6. **Batter** the fillet, lift by the tail, drip 2-3 sec.
7. **Lower head-first; hold the tail submerged 5-8 sec before releasing.** Sets the crust evenly, prevents sticking.³
8. **2 fillets max per batch.** Fry **5-7 min** for a 2.5-3 cm fillet, turn at 3 min. Done when uniform deep gold, audibly crackles, fish reads **60-63°C** internal.⁴
9. **Drain 5 sec above pot, transfer straight to a wire rack.** Never paper towels. Never foil.⁵
10. **Salt with Maldon within 5 sec of leaving oil**, from 20 cm height.⁵
11. **Hold in 90°C oven** on the rack, uncovered, between batches (max 10 min).
12. **Serve on warm plates** with lemon wedges, tartare, malt vinegar at the table.

---

## CRITICAL POINTS

¹ **Cold dry fish into hot oil.** Wet fish = batter slides off. Brine seasons through; the dry-down lets the dredge bond.

² **Mix batter at the last second.** Gluten forms the moment flour meets liquid; CO₂ leaves the moment beer is open. Rested 10 min = half-dead. Maximum gas, minimum gluten = lacy crust.

³ **Hold submerged before releasing.** First 5-8 sec is when the crust sets. Drop and release immediately — it sinks, sticks, tears.

⁴ **Two fillets per batch in a 4 L pot.** More than two crashes oil below 165°C; batter absorbs oil before it sets — greasy fish.

⁵ **Wire rack + Maldon within 5 sec.** Paper towels trap steam, soften the underside in 90 sec. Salt bonds to hot oily crust in the first 5 sec only. Foil is the enemy.

---

## YACHT-GALLEY NOTES

- **Never fry underway** — hot oil + motion = fire. In port only.
- **Oil disposal:** strain through coffee filter into a sealed bottle, hold for shore disposal. **Never overboard** (MARPOL).
- **Pre-portioned dry batter bags** — 175 g vacuum bags (each = 4 fillets), shelf-stable 1 month. Add cold beer + vodka at service.
- **Freezer:** 200 ml vodka lives there permanently. Pollock IQF fillets thaw 24 hr in fridge, brine as fresh.
- **Do not freeze cooked fish.** Fry to order. No exceptions.
