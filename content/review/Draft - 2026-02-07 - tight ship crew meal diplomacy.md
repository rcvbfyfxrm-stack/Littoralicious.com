# Crew Meal Diplomacy
*Category: Workflow / Crew Integration*

**The Pain:**
You cook 3 meals a day for crew AND multi-course service for guests. The crew returns from leave with a new TikTok diet. The Ukrainian stew wants roast pork and potatoes every night. The deckhand is doing carnivore. The second stew just went vegan. You are one chef. You have one galley. You have finite hours.

**The Protocol:**
1. Establish dietary terms at embarkation, not mid-season. When new crew join, get their genuine allergies and intolerances in writing (medical documentation for allergies, verbal for preferences). State clearly what will and will not be accommodated. Allergies: non-negotiable, always accommodated. Medical conditions (coeliac, diabetes): accommodated. Lifestyle diets adopted from social media last Tuesday: not accommodated.
2. Cook one crew meal with built-in flexibility. Base protein + starch + vegetable, with a salad bar or grain bowl station crew can customise. A braised chicken thigh with rice and roasted vegetables satisfies most of the boat. The fussy crew member adds hot sauce. The vegan gets the vegetables, the rice, and a separate protein you batch-prepped on Monday (roasted chickpeas, marinated tofu, bean stew -- rotated weekly, 30 minutes of prep for 5 days of cover).
3. Set a ketchup-and-Nutella boundary. Stock both. Do not take it personally. The crew member who puts ketchup on your slow-braised short rib is not insulting you -- they are telling you they want ketchup. Ego is the enemy here as much as it is with guest food. Provide condiments. Move on.
4. Batch-prep 3 crew meal components every Monday during guest meal prep downtime: one grain/starch (5 kg rice pilaf, roasted potatoes, couscous), one sauce (2 L bolognese, curry base, tomato sugo), one vegetable prep (roasted root vegetables, blanched greens, pickled onions). Total time: 90 minutes. This gives you a 15-minute assembly window for crew lunch and dinner the rest of the week.
5. Push back once, clearly, in writing if needed. When a crew member requests a bespoke diet that requires separate cooking (keto bread daily, full carnivore protocol, raw food), respond to the captain or chief stew, not the crew member: "This dietary request requires approximately X additional hours per week. I can accommodate it if Y is adjusted." Let management decide. That is their job.

**Why It Works:**
The single biggest source of crew meal friction is ambiguity. When expectations are undefined, every meal becomes a negotiation. The protocol above eliminates negotiation by establishing rules at embarkation and building a system that handles 90% of variation through structure rather than improvisation. Batch prep on Monday converts a daily time drain into a weekly investment. A 90-minute Monday session replaces five 30-minute scrambles -- same total time, but the cognitive load shifts from reactive to planned.

**The Proof:**

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Daily crew meal prep time | 30-45 min per meal (reactive) | 10-15 min per meal (assembly from batch) |
| Dietary complaints per week | 3-5 (unmanaged expectations) | 0-1 (clear terms set at embarkation) |
| Chef stress from crew requests | High (every request is a negotiation) | Low (system handles variation; exceptions escalated to management) |

**The Catch:**
This protocol requires captain/management buy-in on the dietary boundary. If the captain overrides you and says "just make the keto bread," you make the keto bread. Document the additional workload. The goal is not to win the argument — it is to make the cost of accommodation visible so that it becomes a management decision rather than an invisible burden absorbed by the chef. Also: some crew are genuinely difficult and no system fixes that. One chef's advice — "Throw the 2 fussy crew overboard when you leave port" — is not operationally viable, but the sentiment is universal.

**The Monday Batch List**

Do this every Monday. 90 minutes. Covers the week.

| Component | Quantity | Stores For | Examples |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Grain/starch | 5 kg cooked | 5 days | Rice pilaf, roasted potatoes, couscous, pasta salad |
| Sauce | 2 litres | 5 days | Bolognese, curry base, tomato sugo, chimichurri |
| Vegetable prep | 3 kg prepped | 5 days | Roasted roots, blanched greens, pickled onions, slaw |
| Vegan protein | 1 kg | 5 days | Roasted chickpeas, marinated tofu, bean stew |

Assembly at meal time: 10-15 minutes. Protein from that day's service prep + one grain + one veg + sauce. Salad bar stays stocked. Condiments out. Done.
