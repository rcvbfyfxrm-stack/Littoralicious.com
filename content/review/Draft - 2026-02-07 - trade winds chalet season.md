# Chalet Season: The Other Gig That Fills the Off-Season Gap

*February 2026 · Alps — France, Switzerland, Austria*

**The Development:**
Alpine chalet chef work has become the default off-season income stream for yacht chefs who don't want to cross the Atlantic. The format is straightforward: private chalets hosting 8-12 guests per week, typically Saturday to Saturday, with breakfast and dinner service. Lunch is on the mountain. The season runs December through April, with peak demand from Christmas through late February. Two booking platforms dominate the yacht-to-chalet pipeline: AmandineChefs.com handles the bulk of placement in the French Alps, while direct agency bookings cover Switzerland and Austria.

**Who's Affected:**
Any yacht chef between Mediterranean and Caribbean seasons looking for structured, well-paid winter work. Non-EU passport holders considering Swiss placements need to pay particular attention to permit requirements.

**What It Means:**
The pay is competitive. Freelance chalet chefs in the French Alps command EUR 300/day for breakfast and dinner service with a maximum of 12 guests — a workload that most yacht chefs would consider light duty. Salaried positions in Courchevel have been reported at EUR 5,500/month, which multiple chefs in the community consider exceptional for the market. The provisioning environment is fundamentally different from maritime work: Alpine suppliers are reliable but limited in range, and everything costs more at altitude. In St Moritz, Bianchi is the go-to provisioner. Matteo Remorgida is a known local contact for sourcing and logistics. Expect to learn regional staples — chocolate pear tarte is the traditional local cake in the Engadin, and guests expect it.

| Season | December – April (peak: Christmas – late February) |

**Your Move:**
If you're planning chalet season for winter 2026-27, register with AmandineChefs.com now — the best placements fill by September. If you hold a non-EU passport and are considering Switzerland, confirm your work permit situation before accepting. Swiss labour inspectors do check chalets, and getting caught working illegally will create problems that follow you back to yachting.
