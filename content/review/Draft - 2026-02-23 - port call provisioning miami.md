# Provisioning Miami & Fort Lauderdale: The One-Page Playbook

*The best provisioning city in the western hemisphere. Zero import tax, massive wholesale infrastructure, every cuisine represented. If your season starts here or you transit through, load everything you can carry.*

**Port type: Major hub.** Agent markup in this port runs 15-18%. On a USD 15,000 provision, self-sourcing saves USD 2,200-2,700. The wholesale infrastructure is so good here that using an agent is paying someone to do what you can do better yourself — unless you genuinely have zero time.

The single most important thing to know: **Costco Business Center is not regular Costco.** The Business Center in Doral carries restaurant-format packaging — 5kg flour bags, case lots, portion-controlled proteins. Make the extra 20-minute drive. This distinction alone changes your entire provision.

---

Work backwards from charter day. D-4: dry store + online orders placed. D-3: wholesale meat + dairy run. D-2: ethnic, wine, specialty. D-1: fish market at 07:00, produce, bread. D-0: ultra-perishables only.

### General / One-Stop

**Online:** **Instacart** — Pulls from Costco, Whole Foods, Publix. Schedule delivery to the marina. Useful for the general grocery run when you cannot leave the boat.

**In person:** **Costco Business Center** (Doral) — Restaurant-format packaging: 5kg flour bags, case lots, portion-controlled proteins, bulk dairy. One run here covers 40% of your dry store and pantry.

> **The move:** Regular Costco sells consumer packs. The Business Center sells professional packs. The difference in value and format is significant. Also: **Restaurant Depot membership is free** — bring boat registration, a business card, and the captain's ID. One card covers every Restaurant Depot in the US.

---

### Fish & Seafood

**Online:** **Browne Trading** (Portland, ME) — Premium day-boat fish, caviar, smoked fish. Overnight ship to Miami. For guest-level seafood when local catch is not what you need.

**In person:** **Captain Jim's Fish Market** (North Miami Beach) — Local catch, stone crab (Oct-May), Florida lobster. The fishmongers know yacht chefs. Arrive before 08:00 for the best selection.

> **The move:** Haulover Marina (Sunny Isles) — boats come in at noon and 17:00. Buy directly off the boat. Cash preferred. Yellowtail snapper, mahi, swordfish, tuna at 40-60% below retail. Build the relationship — they will call you when they catch something special.

---

### Meat & Protein

**Online:** **Halpern's** — Wholesale account. 2,000+ products across beef and seafood. This is where provisioning agents source from. Go direct and skip the 15-18% middleman. Since 1966, Florida distribution.

**In person:** **Restaurant Depot** (FTL, Pompano, Miami, Medley) — USDA Prime and Choice at wholesale. Guest and crew proteins, dairy, dry goods. One stop for 60% of your provision.

> **The move:** For guest-level cuts, **Josh's Premium Meats** (SW 57th Ave) specialises in Australian and Japanese Wagyu with same-day Miami-Dade delivery. For pork specifically, **Snake River Farms** ships American Wagyu beef and Kurobuta pork frozen and vac-packed — order 3 days ahead. For premium deli, caviar, foie gras, and charcuterie: **Marky's wholesale portal** (wholesale.markys.com), not the retail site.

---

### Bread & Bakery

**Online:** **Zak the Baker** (Wynwood) — Artisan sourdough, challah, focaccia. Order 48h ahead for volume. Delivers.

**In person:** Same location for pickup if you prefer to inspect.

> **The move:** Stock up on Caputo flour and bread flour at Restaurant Depot. Once you hit the Caribbean, 12%+ protein flour is hard to find and expensive. Buy 10-20kg here.

---

### Produce & Fruit

**In person:** **Robert Is Here** (Homestead) — Tropical exotics: mamey, sapodilla, jackfruit, starfruit, dragon fruit. Unavailable elsewhere in the US. Worth the drive for guest-level tropical fruit that impresses.

**Bulk:** **Restaurant Depot** for standard produce at wholesale prices.

> **The move:** For guest garnish and tasting menu produce — microgreens, edible flowers, specialty vegetables — **The Chef's Garden** (Ohio) ships overnight. 400-acre regenerative farm. For luxury ingredients (truffles, specialty mushrooms, Japanese wasabi): **Regalis Foods** ships in 2 days.

---

### Dry Store & Pantry

**Online:** **Amazon Fresh** — Order D-5, arrives next day. Good for specialty items and anything you cannot find at wholesale.

**In person:** **Costco Business Center** (Doral) — Case lots of oils, vinegars, flour, sugar, pasta. One run.

> **The move:** Place Instacart and Amazon Fresh orders on D-5. Do the Costco Business Center run D-4 morning. This leaves the rest of the week free for the market runs that actually require your eyes and hands.

---

### Ethnic & Specialty

**In person:** **168 Market** (Hialeah) — Full Asian supermarket. Lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, rice noodles, miso, dashi, nori, Thai curry pastes, wonton wrappers. The only reliable large-scale Asian source in South Florida.

**Also:** **Sedano's** (multiple locations) — Cuban/Latin. Plantains, yuca, black beans, mojo, sazon, achiote.

> **The move:** 168 Market is the last reliable large Asian source before the Caribbean. Buy your entire Asian pantry here in bulk. What you do not buy now, you will not find at the next port.

---

### Wine & Spirits

**In person:** **Total Wine** (multiple locations) — Broadest selection in South Florida. Online ordering available.

> **The move:** If heading international, duty-free spirits at the port. Buy all spirits for the Caribbean leg here — prices only go up from here.

## FREEZER-READY

Most of what you buy gets frozen. The suppliers who vac-pack, portion, and blast-chill before you collect save hours of galley prep and preserve quality.

| Supplier | Vac-Pack | Portioning | Blast Chill / Pre-Frozen | Lead Time |
|----------|----------|------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **Wild Fork Foods** | Yes (most products) | Individual portions standard | Products arrive frozen from facility | 48h for full catalogue |
| **Snake River Farms** | Yes | Individual cuts standard | Shipped frozen | 3 days |
| **Halpern's** | TBC — confirm on account setup | TBC | TBC | TBC |
| **Josh's Premium Meats** | TBC — ask on Wagyu orders | TBC | TBC | Same day Miami-Dade |
| **Browne Trading** | TBC — ask for larger orders | TBC | Ships overnight on ice | Overnight |
| **Restaurant Depot** | No (self-service) | No | No | Walk in |

> **What to ask every protein supplier:** (1) Can you vac-pack? (2) Can you portion — whole fillet AND individual cuts? (3) What lead time for packed orders? (4) Blast chiller available? A supplier who vac-packs and blast-chills is worth a 10% premium.

**Buy here, freeze for later:** Wild Fork and Snake River Farms arrive already frozen and vac-packed — best quality for freezer loading. For proteins heading to the Caribbean, these two are your cleanest freezer-ready option.
**Already frozen, high quality:** Wild Fork (broad range, crew to Wagyu), Snake River Farms (American Wagyu beef, Kurobuta pork).
**Do not freeze from here:** Fish. Buy fish fresh at the next port. Frozen fish from Miami competes poorly with fresh local catch in the Caribbean.

## NO TIME — THE AGENT

Sometimes you dock at 14:00 and charter starts at 18:00 the next day.

**Best agent:** **Yacht Provisions USA** — Full-service provisioning. They source from Halpern's and the same wholesale suppliers, add 15-18% markup, and deliver to the marina. On a USD 15,000 provision, that is USD 2,250-2,700.

> **The move:** Use the agent only when your charter starts in 24 hours and you have not left the boat. Miami's wholesale infrastructure is so accessible that the agent markup is harder to justify here than anywhere else.

**Other chandlers:** Yacht Chandlers (29 years, daily delivery FTL/Miami/WPB), National Marine Suppliers (direct farm/ranch), ODP — On Demand Provisions (run by an exec yacht chef), Just Provisions (gangway delivery FTL).

**Red flags:** Any agent who cannot tell you exactly where they source proteins. If they are vague, they are marking up Restaurant Depot.

## THE BOTTOM LINE

Miami is the provisioning capital of the Americas. Zero import tax, wholesale infrastructure everywhere, every cuisine and quality tier covered from crew-grade to A5 Wagyu. The single best move is getting the Costco Business Center and Restaurant Depot memberships on day one — between them, they cover 60-70% of any provision at wholesale prices. Before you leave, load flour, Asian pantry staples, and frozen vac-packed proteins for the next three ports. Prices and suppliers shift season to season — verify before committing.
