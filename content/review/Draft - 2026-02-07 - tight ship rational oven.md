# The Galley Equipment Verdict: What 1,300 Yacht Chefs Actually Use

*Category: Equipment*

**The Pain:**
You inherit a galley. Half the equipment was specified by an interior designer who has never plated a dish. The Thermomix dies mid-charter. The Miele oven trips the generator. The KitchenAid struggles with bread dough. You spend the first week fighting machines instead of cooking.

This is not a buying guide. This is a professional consensus document — built from polls, debates, and years of collective daily use across two WhatsApp groups with 1,300+ working yacht chefs. No affiliate links. No sponsored opinions. The equipment that survives a season at sea earns its place here. Everything else gets thrown in the lazarette.

---

## Combi Ovens

### The Verdict: Rational iCombi Pro

**Poll result: 33 votes Rational, 0 votes Convotherm.** Not close. Not debatable.

The iCombi Pro's core advantage is its self-adjusting cooking intelligence — the unit monitors cavity humidity, probe temperature, and browning level simultaneously, adjusting heat and steam without manual intervention. For a sole chef running service while prepping the next course, this is a second pair of hands.

**Marine version:** Exists. Specify it. The marine-spec latching system prevents door swing at sea — stiff by design, do not force them. Maintenance: descale every 50 operating hours in salt-air environments. The steam generator is the primary failure mode on yachts.

**The recipe that proved it:** Carrot cake at 130°C, fan speed 3/5, 50 minutes. No steam. Level crumb, even browning, no dome. Worked because Rational's 5-speed fan granularity allows precise convection without surface drying.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Rational iCombi Pro** | The standard | 33/33 poll. Self-adjusting. Marine version available. Global service network. One independent engineer has serviced every generation since 1996 |
| MKN SpaceCombi | Only alternative | For built-in galleys where the Rational footprint does not fit |
| Convotherm | Avoid | "Non-intuitive touchscreen." "Should come with drywall to punch" |
| Miele | Avoid | "Fuck Miele" is not editorial — it is professional consensus. Ovens failing, cooktops shattering, dual ovens dying simultaneously |
| MyChef | Avoid | "How much do we all hate these?" Universal agreement |
| Unox | Avoid | "Quite horrible" |

**The catch:** The Rational is not cheap. Marine version commands a premium. On boats under 40m, the full-size unit may not fit — the 6-grid half-size is the compromise, and it is a real compromise for guest counts above 8. And no combi oven fixes bad electrical infrastructure. Verify your available amperage before ordering.

## Blenders & Food Processors

### The Verdict: Heinzelmann Chef-X

16,000 units sold in the EU. No touchscreen. Zero safety features. You cannot destroy them.

The Chef-X is a commercial machine built for chefs who need raw power and zero interface friction. No programmes, no presets, no app connectivity. A dial and a button. It handles soups, emulsifications, nut butters, and frozen fruit with the same indifference. 220V only — works on every European-flagged vessel without a transformer.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Heinzelmann Chef-X** | The standard | Indestructible. No touchscreen. 220V. 16,000 units sold |
| Robot Coupe Cook | Professional alternative | 1800W industrial, 4L, built-in scraping spatula. Solid if you need integrated heating |
| Thermomix | Dead to me | "Built for Karens." "Got worse with every version for chefs." The founder of Superyacht Galley Designs: "Thermomix is dead to me" |
| Ninja Creami | Budget Pacojet | "A Temu Paco." Useful for ice cream and sorbets if the real thing is out of budget |

**The catch:** The Chef-X has no safety features. This is either its best quality or its worst, depending on whether you respect the machine. The Thermomix's decline is instructive — each generation added consumer features (touchscreen, WiFi, guided cooking) while reducing the raw processing capability that chefs need. The Chef-X went the other direction.

## Mixers

### The Verdict: Hobart 10L

The Hobart 10L is the galley mixer. Not the most exciting answer. The correct one.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Hobart 10L** | The standard | Commercial build. Handles bread dough without straining. Parts available globally |
| Ankarsrum | Bread specialist | Swedish. Excellent for bread specifically. Less versatile than the Hobart for general use |
| Buffalo 10L | Budget option | Functional but the bowl lock breaks. Acceptable if budget is the constraint. Replace the bowl lock pre-emptively |
| KitchenAid | Not for yachts | Consumer build quality marketed at professional prices. Struggles with dense doughs. Motor burns out under sustained commercial use |

**The catch:** The Hobart is heavy. On a vessel where every kilogram is negotiated, this matters. The Ankarsrum is lighter and better for bread but less capable as a general-purpose mixer. If you bake daily and rarely use a paddle attachment, the Ankarsrum wins. For everything else, Hobart.

## Dishwashers

### The Verdict: Hobart

Hobart again. Commercial dishwashers are binary: they either clean everything in 90 seconds or they ruin your service. The Hobart does the former.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Hobart** | The standard | Fast cycle, reliable, parts available, survives marine environment |
| Miele | Ongoing complaints | Domestic build quality in a commercial-demand environment |

**The catch:** A Hobart commercial dishwasher needs a dedicated hot water feed and a dedicated electrical circuit. If your galley was plumbed for a domestic unit, retrofit requires plumbing work. Worth it. A 90-second wash cycle versus a 45-minute domestic cycle is the difference between smooth service and stacking dirty plates on every surface.

## Sous Vide

### The Verdict: PolyScience

The PolyScience immersion circulator is the professional standard. Chefs report 10+ years of daily use with zero failures. That is the entire argument.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **PolyScience** | The standard | Professional grade. 10+ year longevity documented. Precise temperature stability |
| Anova Pro | Acceptable | Consumer-professional crossover. Less durable but functional |
| Fusion Chef | Professional alternative | German-made, precise. Less common in yacht galleys but solid |
| Inkbird | Budget | Does the job. Replace every 2-3 years |

**The catch:** Sous vide at sea adds a variable — vessel motion creates water movement that affects heat distribution in larger baths. Use a vessel with a lid. Secure the circulator with a clamp rated for lateral movement, not the clip that comes in the box.

## Cookware

### The Verdict: De Buyer Carbon Steel

"All day, every day." Carbon steel is lighter than cast iron, heats faster, responds to temperature changes immediately, and develops a non-stick surface through seasoning that improves with use. De Buyer is the French standard — available in every professional kitchen supplier in the Mediterranean.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **De Buyer carbon steel** | The standard | Light, responsive, indestructible, improves with use |
| Cristel | Stainless alternative | French. Use for acidic sauces and anything that strips carbon steel seasoning |
| DeMeyere | Premium stainless | Belgian. 7-ply construction. Heavy but even heat distribution |
| HexClad | Avoid | "Noisy and the black stuff comes off." Marketing exceeds performance |

**The catch:** Carbon steel requires seasoning and maintenance. It will rust if stored wet. In a marine environment with salt air, dry pans immediately after washing, apply a thin oil film, and store with paper towels between stacked pans. A well-maintained carbon steel pan outlasts three non-stick replacements.

## Cutting Boards

### The Verdict: Hasegawa (with Asahi as Backup)

The Hasegawa soft rubber cutting board protects knife edges better than any wood, bamboo, or plastic board. Knife sharpness is a function of two things: the steel and the surface you cut on. The board matters as much as the stone.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Hasegawa** | The standard | Soft rubber compound. Knife-friendly. Professional standard in Japanese kitchens |
| **Asahi** | Close second | Same rubber principle. "Get the deckies to sand them" when they wear |
| Plastic (PE/PP) | For HACCP | Colour-coded boards for allergen compliance. Not for precision knife work |
| Wood (end-grain) | Impractical at sea | Heavy, warps in marine humidity, requires maintenance. Leave at home |

**The catch:** Hasegawa boards stain. Cosmetic, not functional. They also wear down — the surface develops grooves. Unlike plastic boards that need replacing, rubber boards can be sanded flat. An orbital sander with 120-grit, 5 minutes per board, restores the cutting surface. Deckhand work.

## Knife Maintenance

### The Verdict: Leather Strop with Diamond Compound

Not a knife recommendation — a maintenance protocol. The knife you own matters less than how you maintain it.

**The system:** A leather strop loaded with diamond compound for daily maintenance between sharpenings. This is not honing — it is micro-polishing the edge at the existing bevel angle. Five passes per side, once daily, 30 seconds. Chefs who adopted this report extending the interval between stone sharpening from weekly to monthly.

| Tool | Use | Why |
|------|-----|-----|
| **Leather strop + diamond compound** | Daily maintenance | "Changed everything." 30 seconds per knife |
| **Jewelstik diamond steel** | Mid-service touch-ups | Diamond-coated. Removes less material than ceramic |
| Whetstone (1000/3000 grit) | Monthly sharpening | The actual sharpening. Learn the angle. Practice on cheap knives first |
| Antibes knife shop | Quarterly professional service | Known in the fleet. Walk-in. Same-day return |

**The catch:** Diamond compound comes in grits. Start at 1 micron (fine) for maintenance. If you go coarser (6 micron), you are reshaping the edge — that requires technique. The strop must be mounted flat or hung taut. A floppy strop rounds the edge instead of polishing it.

## Pizza Ovens

### The Verdict: Effeuno N4

500°C. At that temperature, a Neapolitan-style pizza bakes in 60-90 seconds. No combi oven can replicate this. For a vessel with a pizza night tradition — most of them — the N4 is the difference between pizza and flatbread with toppings.

| Brand | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Effeuno N4** | The standard | 500°C. Compact. 220V. Fits on a countertop |
| Ooni Volt 2 | Electric alternative | Good for outdoor deck service. Portable. Lower max temperature |
| Forni David | Premium | Italian-made. Larger capacity. For dedicated outdoor cooking stations |

**The catch:** The Effeuno draws 3.5 kW at peak. Verify your circuit can handle it alongside other galley equipment. Store it secured — at 15 kg, it becomes a projectile in rough weather.
