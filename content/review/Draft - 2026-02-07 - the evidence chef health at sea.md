# Chef Health at Sea: Occupational Hazards the Industry Prefers to Ignore

**The Study:**
This piece synthesises data from multiple sources: the Maritime Labour Convention (MLC) 2006 as amended, ISWAN (International Seafarers' Welfare and Assistance Network) reporting, and documented accounts from working yacht chefs. While no single peer-reviewed study focuses exclusively on yacht chef occupational health, the broader maritime occupational health literature -- particularly ITF (International Transport Workers' Federation) data on seafarer working hours and the *International Maritime Health* journal's ongoing research into musculoskeletal disorders among galley workers -- establishes the clinical framework. The anecdotal data below comes from two yacht chef WhatsApp groups (~1,325 combined members, July 2025 - January 2026).

**Key Finding:**
Yacht chefs face a convergence of musculoskeletal, gastrointestinal, and psychological occupational hazards that are structurally enabled by 16-18 hour working days, falsified hours of rest records, and captains who treat medical complaints as inconveniences.

**The Numbers:**

| Risk | Data | Context |
|------|------|---------|
| Working hours | 16-18 hour days documented across both groups | MLC stipulates maximum 14 hours in any 24-hour period and 72 hours in any 7-day period. Falsification of rest records is widespread and openly discussed. |
| Carpal tunnel syndrome | Reported as "widespread" with multiple chefs sharing treatment protocols | Repetitive knife work, gripping, and vibration exposure (blenders, mixers) over years. Prevention discussed more than diagnosis -- the condition is treated as inevitable. |
| Acute illness at sea | One chef documented a hypothyroid crisis: 34.9C body temperature, low blood pressure, fatigue, nausea, first migraine. Captain "incredibly dismissive." | Another chef lost 9.5 kg from undiagnosed Campylobacter infection contracted on board. |
| Mental health | "I've never felt so alone" -- written at 2am on rough seas during a medical crisis | Sole chefs have no departmental colleague. ISWAN provides a 24/7 helpline but awareness is low. |

**What This Means in the Galley:**
Know your rights before you need them. Under MLC Regulation 4.1, you are entitled to health protection and medical care at no cost, including access to a qualified medical doctor at the nearest port. If you are sick or injured at sea, you have up to 16 weeks of medical cover fully paid by the shipowner. This is not a benefit. It is law. The DPA (Designated Person Ashore) exists as your escalation path when a captain dismisses a medical concern -- use it.

For carpal tunnel specifically, the prevention protocol discussed across both groups includes: wearing wrist braces during sleep, maintaining sharp knives (dull blades increase grip force by up to 60%), using thick cutting boards that absorb impact (Hasegawa or Asahi rubber), and investing in custom orthotic insoles from a specialist -- not off-the-shelf inserts. Acupuncture and dry needling were cited by multiple chefs as effective for managing existing symptoms.

**What This Does Not Mean:**
This is not medical advice. Every case cited here is anecdotal. If you have symptoms -- numbness in your hands, persistent fatigue, unexplained weight loss, chest pain -- see a doctor. Not a captain. Not a first mate. A doctor.

**Limitations:**
- No yacht-chef-specific epidemiological study exists; prevalence data is extrapolated from broader maritime and commercial kitchen research
- Self-reported data from WhatsApp groups carries selection bias -- chefs with problems talk about them; healthy chefs don't
- MLC enforcement varies dramatically by flag state; the law on paper and the law in practice are different things

**Your Move:**
Save the ISWAN SeafarerHelp number in your phone now: +44 (0)20 7323 2737. It operates 24/7 in multiple languages. Know your DPA's name and direct contact. If your management company cannot tell you who the DPA is, that is itself a red flag. Read MLC Regulation 4.1 and Standard A4.1 -- it takes ten minutes and it is the document that protects you when nobody else will.

**Source:** Maritime Labour Convention, 2006 (as amended). ISWAN, "SeafarerHelp Annual Report," 2024. ITF, "Seafarer Working Hours," 2023.
