# The Superyacht Chef Competition Circuit and Why Entering Matters

*February 2026 · Monaco / Antigua*

**What Happened:**
Two competitions now anchor the yacht chef calendar. The Monaco Yacht Chef Competition, held alongside the Monaco Yacht Show each September, has become the highest-profile culinary contest in the superyacht sector. The Antigua Charter Yacht Show Concours de Chef, running each December during the charter show, is the Caribbean counterpart — and the only competition where your cooking is judged in context, aboard the vessel you actually work on.

**Who:**
Both competitions are open to working yacht chefs. The Antigua Concours de Chef divides entrants by vessel size: 125 ft (38 m) and under, 126-179 ft (38-55 m), and 180 ft (55 m) and above. The 2025 edition ran under the theme "Journey of Wellness," requiring competitors to build menus around nutritional intent without sacrificing the standard expected aboard a charter yacht. Monaco draws competitors from private and charter vessels across the Mediterranean fleet.

**Why This Matters:**
Yacht chefs operate in professional isolation. There is no brigade to benchmark against, no peer review during service, no external standard beyond whether the guest complained. Competitions are the only structured environment where your work is assessed by judges who understand the constraints — galley size, provisioning limitations, crew of one or two — and evaluate accordingly. A placing or win becomes a credential that agents, captains, and management companies recognise. It is one of the few ways to demonstrate range and execution under pressure to people who were not on your boat when you cooked. Beyond the CV line, competitors consistently report that the preparation process — building a menu to a brief, sourcing under constraints, executing for a panel — sharpens skills that atrophy when you cook for the same owners month after month.

**The Detail Worth Knowing:**
The Antigua Concours de Chef judges evaluate aboard the competing yacht. They see your galley. They see your storage. They see the equipment you work with. This is not a hotel kitchen with a sponsor's range and unlimited prep space. The constraint is the point, and the judges know it. That structural honesty is what separates these competitions from shore-based contests where yacht chefs are competing on someone else's terms.

**Source:** Antigua Charter Yacht Show, "Concours de Chef 2025 — Journey of Wellness." Monaco Yacht Show official programme, September 2025. Yacht chef community reports from both events.
