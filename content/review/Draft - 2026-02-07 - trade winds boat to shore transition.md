# From Boat to Shore: Transitioning to Private Chef Work

*February 2026 · Global*

**The Development:**
Every yacht chef eventually asks the question: what comes after the boat? The galley-to-shore pipeline is well-established but poorly documented. Chefs leave yachting for private household work, villa season cooking, estate management roles, or independent freelance operations — but the transition is rarely clean, and the financial and lifestyle adjustments catch people off guard. Data from both major yacht chef WhatsApp groups shows this question recurring monthly, with experienced chefs sharing the same advice in fragments that have never been consolidated.

**Who's Affected:**
Yacht chefs at any career stage considering shore-based private work — whether as a permanent exit from yachting, a seasonal supplement during off-rotation, or a trial run before committing to the transition. Also relevant for chefs already freelancing between contracts who want to build a stable shore-based client base.

**What It Means:**
The skills transfer directly. A yacht chef who can cook for 12 guests with 2 hours' notice, manage provisioning logistics across three countries, and adapt to dietary restrictions mid-service is overqualified for most private household positions. The challenge is not competence — it is visibility. Shore-based private clients do not recruit from Dockwalk or yacht crew agencies. They use different platforms, different networks, and different language. The chefs who transition successfully are the ones who understand this before they leave the boat.

**The Pathways:**

### 1. Villa and Holiday Rental Work

The fastest entry point. Vacation rental management companies in the South of France, Ibiza, Tuscany, the Cotswolds, and the Caribbean contract chefs for guest stays ranging from 3 days to 3 weeks. The work is structurally identical to charter — you cook for guests who expect high-end service in a private setting, you manage provisioning, and you leave when they do.

**Key contact:** AmandineChefs.com handles placements for villa chef work across the South of France. Multiple yacht chefs have used this agency as their first shore-based work.

**Day rates:** EUR 250–500 depending on location, guest count, and season. Peak periods (Christmas in St Barths, August in Sardinia) command the higher end.

**How to get in:** Approach vacation rental management companies directly. They manage the properties and need chefs for guest bookings. Your yacht CV and references are directly relevant — translate "12-guest charter" into "villa dining service" and the conversation is the same.

### 2. Private Household (Permanent)

Full-time employment cooking for a single family or principal. This is where yacht chefs often land permanently — the UHNW clients who hire household chefs are frequently the same people who own yachts. The transition sometimes happens organically: you cook for an owner on board, they ask you to cook at their London house, and you never go back to sea.

**Agencies:**
- **Estate Jobs** (UK/International) — headhunter-style placements for UHNW households
- **Wellington Agency** (UK) — private staff recruitment, strong in London and country estates
- **Greycoat Lumleys** (UK) — long-established domestic staff agency
- **Polo & Tweed** (UK/International) — private household recruitment

**Salary benchmarks:**

| Location | Role | Salary Range | Notes |
|----------|------|-------------|-------|
| London | Private chef, single household | GBP 50,000–80,000/year | Plus accommodation in some roles. The 25k GBP offer circulated in groups was "quite a poor offer" — do not accept below market |
| Saudi Arabia / Gulf | Private chef, UHNW family | $10,000+/month | Car, accommodation, flights, and insurance provided. Tax-free. Minimum 12-month contract |
| San Francisco / NYC | Private chef, tech/finance | $150,000–250,000/year | Extremely high cost of living. California tax burden noted as "50 cents on the dollar." Verify net take-home before accepting |
| French Riviera | Private chef, seasonal villa | EUR 5,000–8,000/month | Seasonal (May–September). Often includes accommodation |
| Alps (chalet season) | Chef, ski chalet | EUR 5,000–5,500/month or EUR 300/day freelance | December–April. Breakfast and dinner service only, max 12 guests. Courchevel at EUR 5,500/month considered exceptional |

### 3. Freelance / Independent

Building your own client base through direct relationships, local networks, and platforms. This is the highest-earning path long-term but the slowest to establish.

**How to start:**
- **Sous-chef for established private chefs.** Work under someone who already has the clients. Learn the shore-based service rhythm, which is different from charter: longer lead times, more control over provisioning, different plating expectations.
- **Join local Facebook groups.** Private chef requests frequently appear in neighbourhood, expat, and luxury property groups. This is unglamorous but effective — a villa manager in Ibiza posting "need a chef for next Thursday" is how freelance work begins.
- **Register with multiple agencies simultaneously.** No single agency has enough work to keep a freelancer busy year-round. Spread across 3–5 agencies covering different regions.
- **Build a minimal web presence.** A single-page website with your CV, three photos of actual plated food (not stock images), and a contact form. Shore-based clients Google you — yacht clients do not. This is the biggest operational difference.

### 4. Regional Hotspots

The markets where demand for former yacht chefs is highest, based on community data:

- **Saudi Arabia / Gulf States:** Fastest-growing market. Demand for Western-trained private chefs has increased significantly since 2023. Tax-free income. Cultural adjustment required — research local expectations before accepting.
- **London:** Consistent demand, competitive market. Estate Jobs and Wellington Agency are the primary channels.
- **New York City:** High salaries offset by high cost of living and aggressive tax burden. Network through hospitality industry contacts.
- **San Francisco / Silicon Valley:** Tech wealth drives demand. Salaries up to $250,000 but California state tax reduces net income substantially.
- **South of France:** Seasonal villa work feeds into permanent placements. Start with AmandineChefs.com.

**Your Move:**
If you are considering the transition, start before you leave the boat. Register with 2–3 agencies while still employed — the yacht CV is your strongest asset, and agencies respond faster to working chefs than to unemployed ones. Do one villa season as a trial before committing to a permanent shore position. The work is familiar. The lifestyle adjustment — fixed address, consistent schedule, no crew, no vessel — is the part that catches people.

**Source:** Yacht chef community data, Chefs Helping Chefs and Yacht Chef Group WhatsApp, July 2025–January 2026. Agency details verified via institutional websites, February 2026.
