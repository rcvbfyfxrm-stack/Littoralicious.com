# The Provisioning Bible: Mediterranean

*Port-by-port. Supplier-by-supplier. No chandler markup required.*

Every supplier listed here has been recommended by working yacht chefs, most of them multiple times, across both the Chefs Helping Chefs and Yacht Chef Group communities. Where problems have been reported, those are listed too.

The chandler markup across the western Med runs 15-30%. Self-sourcing the bulk of your provision and using a chandler only for logistics and last-mile delivery saves thousands per charter. That is the entire premise of this guide.

---

## Metro Card Logistics

Metro operates differently in each country. Your card from one country does not work in another.

| Country | What You Need | Processing | Notes |
|---------|--------------|------------|-------|
| **Italy** | Italian fiscal code (codice fiscale) | Walk-in | Works at any Metro in Italy. Does NOT work abroad. |
| **France** | Boat registration, crew list, captain's passport | 1-2 days | Max 3 cards per vessel. Bring all documents in person. |
| **Spain** | CIF (company/management tax ID), crew passports, boat registration | 1-2 days | Easier than France, still not walk-in. |
| **Greece** | Boat registration, crew passport | Same day | Relatively straightforward. |

> **Smart tip:** If you are doing a western Med season (Spain → France → Italy), start Metro paperwork at your first port. You need three separate cards. Do not arrive in Antibes assuming your Italian card works. Budget one morning per country for the admin.

**Port type: Major hub.** More yachts provision here than anywhere else in the Med. One of the few ports where you can self-source to Michelin-level standards without a chandler.

### Category Detail

#### Fish & Seafood

**Order online:**
- **Gourmet Deliveries** — Order specific species with 48h notice. They source from local fishermen and wholesale markets. Delivery to STP and Club de Mar.

**Go in person:**
- **Santa Catalina Market** — The working chef's market. Daily local catch. Arrive before 08:00 for best selection.

> **Smart tip:** For day-of sashimi-grade product, go to the market yourself and inspect. For volume or specific species (whole turbot, langoustines for 12), order through Gourmet Deliveries — they can guarantee the order where the market cannot.

#### Meat & Protein

**Order online (WhatsApp):**
- **Simon Jones / The Meat & Fish Society** — Covers Mallorca and Ibiza, ships to Sardinia. Wagyu, dry-aged cuts, premium seafood. Universally praised across both chef groups. WhatsApp your order 72h ahead.

> **Smart tip:** Simon Jones is the single most recommended protein supplier in the entire Med. Contact him before your first Balearics charter. He knows what guest-level means and delivers accordingly. If your next stop is Sardinia, he ships there too.

**Buy here:** Guest-level proteins for the entire Balearics leg
**Skip:** Mercadona for guest meat. It is crew-grade only.

#### General / One-Stop

**Order online:**
- **Gourmet Deliveries** — Most-voted supplier in a recent group poll (16 votes). Broad catalogue, reliable delivery, accommodates last-minute changes. Order online, delivery to STP and Club de Mar.
- **We Supply Yachts** — 10 votes in the same poll. Good range, competitive on staples.

**Go in person:**
- **Mercadona** — Crew-level groceries. Consistent, cheap, good enough. Do not source guest provisions here.
- **Metro Palma** — Spanish Metro card required (see logistics section). Bulk dry goods, dairy, frozen. Professional pricing.

> **Smart tip:** Order guest provision through Gourmet Deliveries (D-3). Do the Metro run for bulk crew and dry store (D-2). Mercadona for crew snacks and basics (D-2 same trip). Simon Jones delivers proteins D-1. Market run D-1 morning for fish, produce, bread. Five contacts, three days, complete provision.

#### Bread & Bakery

**Go in person:**
- **Santa Catalina Market** bakeries — Good quality local bread.

> **Smart tip:** Bread flour sourcing is a perennial headache in the Med. **Caputo flour at 13% protein** is the recommendation from multiple chefs. Available at specialty food suppliers and some Metro locations in Palma. Stock up here — 12%+ protein flour is not consistently available in the western Med.

### Category Detail

#### Meat & Protein

**Go in person:**
- **Mercat del Ninot** — Strong meat counters, less crowded than the tourist markets. Near the Eixample marina facilities.

> **Smart tip:** Barcelona has excellent Iberian pork suppliers. Jamones and embutidos are significantly cheaper bought at source here than anywhere in France or Italy. If your itinerary allows, stock your cured meats in Barcelona. Vacuum-seal and they keep for weeks.

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **Mercat del Ninot** — Good fish counter. Fresh, local, less tourist markup.
- **Mercat de Sant Antoni** — Broader selection, busier. Better for produce than fish.

#### Ethnic & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **Raval neighbourhood** — Barcelona's most diverse quarter. Middle Eastern groceries, South Asian spice shops, Chinese supermarkets within walking distance of each other. Worth a 2-hour walkthrough to stock specialty items.

> **Smart tip:** La Boqueria is for tourists. Forget it. The working chef's markets are Sant Antoni and Ninot. Less spectacle, better prices, no one taking photos of your fish.

### Logistics Note

Yacht berths are spread across Port Vell, Port Olimpic, and OneOcean Port Vell. Confirm with suppliers which marina they deliver to directly.

| Bomba rice (for paella) | Metro or Mercat de Sant Antoni | The right rice at the right price |

## Sardinia (Porto Cervo, Olbia, Cagliari)

**Port type: Thin infrastructure despite wealthy clientele.** Peak season July-August (Costa Smeralda circuit). Plan ahead.

| **Meat** | **Simon Jones** (ships from Palma) | — | Order from Palma. Local guest-grade meat is unreliable. |
| **Bread** | — | Local bakeries (pane carasau is Sardinian) | — |

| **Dry Store** | — | **Metro Olbia** | Italian Metro card required |

### Port-by-Port

**Porto Cervo:** Limited options. Riccardo Fish and Cheese comes to the marina — excellent local cheeses, decent fish. For anything else, order from Palma.

**Olbia:** Better supermarket infrastructure. Metro Olbia serviceable for staples. Fish market near the port — local catch quality is high, variety is seasonal and limited.

**Cagliari:** The best provisioning on the island. Mercato di San Benedetto — exceptional fish, local produce, Sardinian cheeses (pecorino sardo). Stock up here if your itinerary touches southern Sardinia.

> **Smart tip:** Order proteins from Simon Jones in Palma with Sardinia delivery. Do Metro Olbia for dry store and frozen. Use Riccardo at the marina for fish and cheese. Three contacts, complete provision.

| Bottarga di muggine | Cagliari fish market / specialty shops | Mullet roe at source. Best quality, best price. |

### Category Detail

#### Meat & Protein

**Order online:**
- **Io Sono La Chianina** — Specialises in Chianina beef. Multiple chefs describe the T-bones as "insane." Delivery to the port area and marina facilities. Order 48h ahead.

> **Smart tip:** Io Sono La Chianina is to Naples what Simon Jones is to Palma. One phone call, guest-level beef sorted.

**Go in person:**
- **Porta Nolana fish markets** (waterfront area) — Exceptional. Arrive before 08:00 — the best product goes first. Negotiate: prices are flexible, especially for bulk purchases.

> **Smart tip:** If your itinerary has you heading north toward the French Riviera, load up on everything Italian here. San Marzano tomatoes, Gragnano pasta, buffalo mozzarella — all at a fraction of what you pay in France. This is the last great Italian sourcing stop before the border.

### The Pesto Move

> **Smart tip:** If your guests want pesto, make it in Genoa with local product. Ligurian basil, local pine nuts, aged Parmigiano from the market. It will be noticeably superior to anything made elsewhere. Buy extra basil and pine nuts — they do not last, but the memory of this pesto on the guest's plate does.

**La Spezia:** Gateway to Cinque Terre. Small fish market, excellent quality — local anchovies and squid. Limited meat; source in Genoa or order ahead.

**Viareggio:** Metro accessible. Moderate options. For guest-level, use Genoa or Tuscan specialty suppliers who deliver.

## Antibes / Cannes / Nice

**Port type: The centre of the yachting world.** The most developed provisioning ecosystem in the Mediterranean. Every chandler, every supplier, every specialty house has a presence or delivers here.

| **Agent** | **Gourmet Deliveries** | — | Full charter provision. Reliable. The default choice on the Riviera. |

### Category Detail

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **Marche Forville** (Cannes) — The working chef's market on the Riviera. Open Tuesday to Sunday. Arrive early for the best fish. Produce quality consistently high.
- **Antibes fish market** (Port Vauban area) — Smaller, excellent local catch. For volume or specific species, order through Gourmet Deliveries with 48h notice.

> **Smart tip:** Forville is where you build relationships. The fish vendors who see you every week give you first pick, better prices, and a phone call when something exceptional comes in. Invest the time early in the season.

#### Produce & Fruit

**Go in person:**
- **Marche Forville** (Cannes) — Guest-level fruit, vegetables, herbs. Consistently high quality.
- **Cours Saleya** (Nice) — Stronger on Niçoise specialties: socca ingredients, mesclun, fresh herbs, small-batch olive oils.

#### Cheese & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **Fromageries in old Antibes and Cannes** — Exceptional. Epoisses, Banon, aged Comté. Better selection here than almost anywhere in the Med. Buy the day before service for optimal condition.

> **Smart tip:** For guest-level cheese courses, skip the supermarket. The fromageries in old Antibes know how to age and store cheese properly. Tell them when you are serving — they will select at the right ripeness. This is the kind of supplier relationship that makes a cheese course memorable.

## Monaco

**Port type: Expensive top-up only.** The most expensive square kilometre in the Med.

### Quick Map

| Category | Order Online | Go In Person | Smart Tip |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **General** | Order from Nice/Antibes for delivery | **Carrefour Monaco** (emergency only) | Everything costs more. Provision in Nice (30 min) and transport in. |
| **Fish** | From Antibes/Nice suppliers | — | — |
| **Wine & Spirits** | — | **Monaco wine merchants** | World-class wine merchants. Pétrus, Krug, verticals. The one thing Monaco does better. |
| **Agent** | Antibes-based agents | — | Coordinate with Gourmet Deliveries or your Riviera supplier for Monaco delivery |

### The Reality

No Metro. No markets worth visiting. Supermarkets at Monaco prices. For anything beyond emergency top-ups, provision in Nice or Antibes and transport in. The 30-minute drive saves 20-40% on a full provision.

> **Smart tip:** Monaco is where you buy wine for the guest who requests a specific vintage, and nothing else. For food, go to Nice.

### Logistics

Yacht berths at Port Hercule are tight. Delivery access is restricted and time-limited. Coordinate with the port authority and your agent. Loading provisions at certain berths requires advance scheduling.

## Mykonos

**Port type: Consumption island, not production island.** Beautiful, expensive, sparse.

### Quick Map

| Category | Order Online | Go In Person | Smart Tip |

| **General** | **Kostas / Food Galore** (order from Athens) | Small local shops | Do not plan to provision a charter from Mykonos shops |

| **Produce** | — | Local shops (tomatoes, cucumbers, herbs in season Jun-Sep) | Decent in season. Everything else is imported. |
| **Agent** | **Kostas / Food Galore** | — | Order from Athens, delivered to the marina. This is the move. |

> **Smart tip:** Plan backwards from Athens. Order through Kostas with Mykonos delivery. Use the port fishermen for day-of catch and local shops for seasonal produce. Everything else should already be on board or incoming from Athens.

**Port type: Exceptional sourcing city.** Rivals Naples for quality and range, at significantly lower prices.

| **Wine & Spirits** | — | Specialist wine shops (Turkish wine improving rapidly) | — |
| **Agent** | — | Coordinate with your marina agent | Marina infrastructure varies. Agent for delivery logistics only. |

### Category Detail

#### Spices & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **Spice Bazaar** (Mısır Çarşısı) — Bulk spices at a fraction of European prices. Sumac, Urfa pepper, pul biber, fresh kaymak, sujuk, pastırma, pomegranate molasses. Quality is superior to exported versions.

> **Smart tip:** Buy spices in bulk — 6-12 months supply. EUR 3-5/kg here for spices that cost EUR 25-40/kg in France. Vacuum-seal and store. This single shopping trip pays for itself many times over.

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **Kadıköy fish market** (Asian side) — Bosphorus fish: turbot, sea bass, bluefish. Arrive early.
- **Black Sea anchovies (hamsi)** in season (November-February) are extraordinary.

> **Smart tip:** Turkish olive oil from Ayvalık or the Aegean coast is world-class and costs EUR 8-12 per litre for oil that would sell for EUR 30+ in France. Buy 10-20L and store.

### Logistics

Marina infrastructure varies. Coordinate with your agent for delivery access. Traffic in Istanbul can add 90 minutes to a provisioning run. Plan for it.

## Supplier Red Flags

These have been flagged repeatedly across the yacht chef community. Do your own due diligence, but consider yourself warned.

**BFG Provisions** — Multiple reports of outstanding debts to yacht chefs and boats. Proceed with extreme caution.

**Yachting Spirit** — Reported 20% markup applied to the total bill (not per-item, on the invoice total). This is on top of already-marked-up per-item pricing. Effective markup can exceed 35-40%. Verify invoices line by line.

**Gibraltar / Morrison's** — Supermarket manager reported as actively hostile to yacht provisioning. If you need to provision in Gibraltar, use Eroski or Mercadona across the border in La Línea (Spain) instead.

**Delicioso (Palma)** — Mercadona-grade products at premium prices. Not worth the markup.

## The Bottom Line

The Med rewards preparation. The chef who spends two hours before arrival placing advance orders and coordinating Metro access saves the boat thousands per charter — and gets better product.

Build relationships. The suppliers listed here are real people running real businesses. The ones who get repeat yacht business offer better pricing, better communication, and better problem-solving when your charter brief changes 36 hours before embarkation.

Prices, contacts, and quality shift season to season. Verify before you commit. But the fundamental map — where to buy what, where to stock up, where to avoid — changes slowly. Use this as your starting grid and update it as you go.
