# The Provisioning Bible: Caribbean & US East Coast

*Port-by-port. What you can get, what you cannot, and where to load up before it is too late.*

Caribbean provisioning is not Mediterranean provisioning. In the Med, you drive between major food cities with deep agricultural hinterlands and wholesale infrastructure. In the Caribbean, you hop between islands where almost everything is imported, cold chains are unreliable, and a 40% import tax can turn a EUR 12,000 provision into EUR 17,000 before you dock.

Every supplier listed here has been recommended by working yacht chefs provisioning during the 2024-2025 and 2025-2026 seasons. No googled suggestions, no tourism board recommendations.

### The Cardinal Rule

**Provision forward.** Whatever you can source and store at your current port, buy it — because the next island may not have it. In the Caribbean, "the next stop" might not have bread flour, decent butter, or fresh herbs at all.

## Miami / Fort Lauderdale

**Port type: Major hub.** The best provisioning city in the western hemisphere for yacht chefs. Zero import tax, massive wholesale infrastructure, every cuisine represented. If your season starts here or you transit through, load everything you can carry.

### Category Detail

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **Captain Jim's Fish Market** (N Miami Beach) — Local catch, stone crab, Florida lobster. The fishmongers know yacht chefs. Arrive before 08:00 for the best selection.

> **Smart tip:** Miami fish markets sell by the boat — if you see a whole yellowtail or cobia that looks good, buy the whole fish. Price per kg drops significantly and you control the butchery.

#### Meat & Protein

**Order online:**
- **Wild Fork Foods** — Online ordering with home/marina delivery. Broad range from crew-grade to Wagyu. 48h lead time for full catalogue. Multiple retail stores across Miami-Dade if you prefer to inspect.

**Go in person:**
- **Restaurant Depot** — Wholesale membership required (boat registration and business card usually accepted). Guest and crew proteins, dairy, dry goods. One stop for 60% of your provision.

> **Smart tip:** Get the Restaurant Depot membership on your first visit. Bring boat registration, a business card, and the captain's ID. Card is free. This single membership covers every Restaurant Depot in the US.

#### General / One-Stop

**Order online:**
- **Instacart** — Pulls from Costco, Whole Foods, Publix. Schedule delivery to the marina or a nearby address. Useful for the general grocery run when you do not want to leave the boat.

**Go in person:**
- **Costco Business Center** (Doral) — Not the regular Costco. The Business Center carries restaurant-format packaging: 5kg flour bags, case lots, portion-controlled proteins, bulk dairy. This is where you do the heavy lift.

> **Smart tip:** Regular Costco sells consumer packs. The Business Center in Doral sells professional packs. The difference in value and format is significant. Make the extra 20-minute drive.

#### Ethnic & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **168 Market** (Hialeah) — Full Asian supermarket. Lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, rice noodles, miso paste, dashi, nori, Thai curry pastes, wonton wrappers. The only reliable large-scale Asian source in South Florida.
- **Sedano's** — Cuban/Latin groceries. Plantains, yuca, black beans, mojo, sazón, achiote. Multiple locations.

**Buy here:** Asian pantry staples in bulk. These are scarce or absent once you leave the US mainland.
**Skip:** Sushi-grade fish at Asian markets. Go to Captain Jim's.

#### Bread & Bakery

**Order online:**
- **Zak the Baker** (Wynwood) — Artisan sourdough, challah, focaccia. Order 48h ahead for volume. Delivers.

> **Smart tip:** Stock up on Caputo flour and bread flour at Restaurant Depot. Once you hit the Caribbean, 12%+ protein flour is hard to find and expensive.

### Provision Schedule

| When | What | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| **D-4** | Dry store, cleaning, bulk frozen, flour | Costco Business Center + Amazon Fresh delivery |
| **D-3** | Meat, dairy, cheese, frozen proteins | Restaurant Depot + Wild Fork |
| **D-2** | Ethnic pantry, wine, specialty items | 168 Market + Total Wine + specialty runs |
| **D-1** | Fish, produce, bread | Captain Jim's (07:00) + produce market + Zak the Baker |
| **D-0** | Ultra-perishables, herbs, last-minute | Whole Foods or nearest Publix |

> **Smart tip:** Place your Instacart/Amazon Fresh orders on D-5. Do the Costco Business Center run D-4 morning. Restaurant Depot D-3. This leaves D-1 free for the market run — which is the only day you actually need to be out early.

### Price Reality

| Item | Via Agent | Self-Sourced | Saving |
|------|----------|--------------|--------|
| Wagyu striploin /kg | USD 120 | USD 88 @ Wild Fork | ~27% |
| King crab legs /kg | USD 95 | USD 65 @ Restaurant Depot | ~32% |
| Organic mixed greens /case | USD 45 | USD 28 @ Costco | ~38% |

**Bottom line:** Miami is where self-sourcing saves the most. No import tax, wholesale access everywhere, delivery infrastructure that works. Use an agent here only if you genuinely have zero time.

### Local Gems

| Item | Where | Why Here |
|------|-------|----------|
| Stone crab claws (Oct-May) | Captain Jim's | Peak season, direct from boats, half the restaurant price |
| Tropical exotics (mamey, sapodilla, jackfruit) | Robert Is Here, Homestead | Unique tropical fruits grown in South Florida, unavailable elsewhere |
| Cuban bread and pastries | Any Sedano's or Latin bakery | Authentic, cheap, crew will thank you |

### If You Have No Time — The Agent

**Yacht Provisions USA** — Full-service provisioning. They source from the same wholesale suppliers and deliver to the marina. Markup 15-18%. On a USD 15,000 provision, that is USD 2,250-2,700. Worth it only when your charter starts in 24 hours and you have not left the boat.

## San Juan, Puerto Rico

**Port type: Strong hub.** US territory — no customs, no import duty, US supply chains. Better provisioned than any Caribbean island. The Sam's Club and Costco alone make it a viable loading port for the eastern Caribbean.

### Quick Map

| Category | Order Online | Go In Person | Smart Tip |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **General** | Instacart (Costco, Walmart, Econo) | **Costco** (Caguas or Carolina) | Same Costco membership as mainland US |
| **Fish** | — | **La Placita de Santurce** fish vendors / **Pescadería del Atlántico** | Arrive before 08:00. Local red snapper, mahi, lobster. |
| **Meat** | — | **Costco** / **Sam's Club** / **Restaurant Depot** | Restaurant Depot in Carolina has professional cuts |
| **Bread** | Panaderías deliver — call ahead | **Panadería España** (Hato Rey) | Puerto Rican pan sobao and pan de agua are excellent crew bread |
| **Produce** | — | **Plaza del Mercado de Río Piedras** | Best local produce: plantains, avocados, recao, ají dulce, tropical fruit |
| **Dry Store** | Amazon (US shipping, no duty) | **Costco** / **Sam's Club** | Amazon delivers with standard US Prime shipping |
| **Frozen** | — | **Costco** / **Sam's Club** | Load freezer here — frozen goods are 30-50% cheaper than island prices |
| **Ethnic** | — | **Pitusa** (multiple, Dominican/Caribbean) / Asian markets in Santurce | Pitusa for Caribbean staples; limited Asian compared to Miami |
| **Wine & Spirits** | — | **Costco** (spirits) / **Bodegas Compostela** (wine) | Puerto Rico has lower spirits tax than mainland US |
| **Agent** | — | Limited options — self-source is the move here | PR is small enough that self-sourcing is fast |

### Category Detail

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **La Placita de Santurce** — The local market district. Fish vendors sell local catch: red snapper, mahi-mahi, Caribbean lobster. Quality is good when product is fresh. Inspect — cold chain varies by vendor.
- **Pescadería del Atlántico** — More consistent than market vendors. Carries local and imported seafood.

> **Smart tip:** Puerto Rican waters produce excellent local fish. Red snapper here is genuinely local, not frozen-and-thawed imports. Ask for "chillo entero" (whole red snapper) — cheaper per kg than fillets and you control the portioning.

#### Meat & Protein

**Go in person:**
- **Costco / Sam's Club** — US-grade proteins at US prices. USDA Prime and Choice available. This is where you load the freezer for the Caribbean leg.
- **Restaurant Depot** (Carolina) — Professional cuts, bulk pricing, same membership as mainland.

> **Smart tip:** Buy 2-3 weeks of frozen proteins here. Vacuum-seal and blast-freeze everything. Once you leave Puerto Rico heading east, meat quality and availability drop significantly and prices rise 30-50%.

#### General / One-Stop

**Order online:**
- **Amazon Prime** delivers to Puerto Rico at standard US rates, no duty. Order dry goods and specialty items D-5 to D-4.

**Go in person:**
- **Costco** (Carolina or Caguas) — Same card, same prices, same product as mainland US.

> **Smart tip:** Puerto Rico is the last place before the eastern Caribbean where you have full US retail infrastructure with no import tax. Treat it as your Miami alternative if your route goes PR → USVI → BVI → eastern Caribbean.

#### Produce & Fruit

**Go in person:**
- **Plaza del Mercado de Río Piedras** — Local produce at local prices. Plantains (green and ripe), avocados, recao (culantro — not cilantro), ají dulce peppers, breadfruit, yams, tropical fruit. Use what Puerto Rico grows — it is excellent and cheap.

**Buy here:** Recao, ají dulce, local avocados, plantains in bulk, tropical fruit
**Skip:** Imported European produce — pay double what you would in Miami for the same product

#### Dry Store & Pantry

**Order online:**
- **Amazon Prime** — Standard US delivery. No duty. Order non-perishables D-5.

**Go in person:**
- **Costco / Sam's Club** — Bulk dry goods at mainland prices.

> **Smart tip:** Load canned goods, oils, flour, sugar, rice, pasta here. These are the items that cost 40-80% more once you reach islands with import duty. Your storage capacity is the only limit.

### Provision Schedule

| When | What | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| **D-5** | Amazon orders (specialty dry goods) | Online |
| **D-3** | Dry store, frozen, bulk | Costco + Sam's Club |
| **D-2** | Meat, dairy, wine, spirits | Restaurant Depot + Costco + Bodegas Compostela |
| **D-1** | Fish, produce, bread | La Placita (07:00) + Río Piedras market + panadería |
| **D-0** | Last-minute, herbs, ultra-perishables | Pueblo or Econo (nearest supermarket) |

### Price Reality

| Item | In Caribbean (Antigua) | In San Juan | Saving |
|------|----------------------|-------------|--------|
| Butter /kg | USD 18 | USD 9 @ Costco | ~50% |
| USDA Choice striploin /kg | USD 55 | USD 32 @ Costco | ~42% |
| Bread flour 5kg | USD 14 | USD 6 @ Sam's Club | ~57% |

**Bottom line:** San Juan gives you US wholesale pricing without crossing to the mainland. If your route touches Puerto Rico, treat it as a major loading port. The savings over provisioning the same items in the eastern Caribbean are 30-50%.

### Local Gems

| Item | Where | Why Here |
|------|-------|----------|
| Recao (culantro) | Río Piedras market | Caribbean herb that adds depth to everything — pennies per bunch here |
| Ají dulce peppers | Río Piedras market | Sweet Caribbean pepper, no heat, extraordinary aroma — unavailable off-island |
| Puerto Rican coffee (Yaucono, Alto Grande) | Any supermarket | Excellent quality, cheap, guests love the story |
| Ron del Barrilito | Specialty liquor shops | Three-star aged rum. Serious sipping rum that impresses guests who think they know Caribbean rum |

## St Maarten / SXM

**Port type: Major hub.** The best-provisioned island in the Caribbean. Duty-free on the Dutch side. This is your loading port — stock up here for the entire first leg of your Caribbean itinerary.

### Quick Map

| **Meat** | **Alan Guichard** (WhatsApp order) | **Alan Guichard** (butcher) | WhatsApp your order 72h ahead. Guest-level proteins rare in the Caribbean — this is the source |
| **Bread** | — | **Carrefour bakery** (French side) | French-standard bakery. Stock baguettes and freeze for the leg |
| **Produce** | — | **Garden Market** (Dutch side) | Best produce on the island by Caribbean standards |
| **Dry Store** | **Carrefour** online / **Cost U Less** delivery | **Cost U Less** | Bulk warehouse — Costco format. Dry goods, cleaning, crew provisions |
| **Frozen** | — | **Cost U Less** / **Carrefour** | Load freezer here. Next islands have limited frozen range |
| **Ethnic** | — | **Carrefour** (French side, Asian aisle) | Limited but functional Asian selection — lemongrass, soy, coconut milk |

| **Agent** | **Epicerie De Marie / Stephan** | — | The consensus: "this guy is the best." Specialty, European imports, delivers to marinas |

### Category Detail

#### Fish & Seafood

**Go in person:**
- **Fish market** (Simpson Bay side, just past the bridge) — Local catch, variable daily. Arrive before 07:00. Cold chain discipline varies by vendor — inspect carefully.

> **Smart tip:** Ask what came in that morning. Do not buy anything that has been sitting. If the market is thin, Stephan at Epicerie De Marie can source guest-level seafood with 48h notice.

**Buy here:** Whatever is fresh and local that day
**Skip:** Pre-ordering specific species — this is a local catch market, not a wholesaler

#### Meat & Protein

**Order online (WhatsApp):**
- **Alan Guichard** — Butcher. Quality meat sourcing that is rare anywhere in the Caribbean. Guest-level proteins: dry-aged, specialty cuts. WhatsApp your order 72h ahead.

**Go in person:**
- **Carrefour** (French side) — European-standard meat counter. Decent for crew proteins, chicken, pork. Not guest-level.

> **Smart tip:** Alan Guichard is the Simon Jones of the Caribbean. Build this relationship on arrival. For guest charters in the eastern Caribbean, this is your primary protein source — order here even if you are picking up in a different port.

#### General / One-Stop

**Order online:**
- **Carrefour** (French side) — Has an ordering system for bulk and specialty items. Ask at the service desk or order online. Place your order 48h before you need it. They prepare the order — you collect at the desk. Saves 2-3 hours of walking the aisles.

**Go in person:**
- **Carrefour** (French side) — The closest thing to a European supermarket in the Caribbean. European butter, cream, cheese, cured meats, baking supplies, cleaning products. This is where you do the general grocery run.
- **Cost U Less** (Dutch side) — Bulk warehouse format. Dry goods, cleaning supplies, crew provisions, bulk beverages. Not for guest product.

> **Smart tip:** Do Carrefour online order for guest groceries (D-3). Do Cost U Less in person for bulk crew/dry store (D-3 same day — they are 15 minutes apart). Two stops, one morning, 70% of your general provision done.

#### Dry Store & Pantry

**Order online:**
- **Carrefour online** — Bulk flour, sugar, oils, pasta, canned goods. European brands.

**Go in person:**
- **Cost U Less** — Case lots, bulk rice, bulk canned goods. Cheapest per-unit prices on the island.

> **Smart tip:** SXM is duty-free on the Dutch side. This means dry goods here cost 30-50% less than on any island with import tax. Fill every locker. Your storage capacity is the only limit. Whatever you do not buy here, you will pay more for at every subsequent port.

#### Ethnic & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **Carrefour** (French side, Asian/ethnic aisle) — Limited but functional: soy sauce, coconut milk, rice noodles, curry pastes, lemongrass (sometimes fresh, usually paste).
- **Chef's Market** — Curated toward professional kitchens. Specialty items, harder-to-find imports.

**Buy here:** Whatever Asian or specialty pantry staples you can find — stock up. This is the last reliable source before you hit islands with nothing.
**Skip:** Fresh Asian herbs (better sourced in Miami or Panama)

**Bottom line:** SXM duty-free pricing is the single biggest advantage in Caribbean provisioning. A EUR 10,000 provision self-sourced here saves EUR 3,000-5,000 compared to the same provision on any taxed island.

| Item | Where | Why Here |
|------|-------|----------|
| French cheese and charcuterie | Carrefour French side | European import prices without Caribbean markup — load the cheese fridge |

**Epicerie De Marie / Stephan** — The most recommended supplier in the Caribbean across both chef groups. Specialty items, European imports, quality control to Med standards. Delivers to marinas. **LuxC'mar Yacht Services** — Full-service provisioning. Handles logistics-heavy, multi-vendor orders. More structured, less personal than Epicerie De Marie. Use when you need one company to coordinate the entire provision.

**Go in person:**
- **Aris Fish** — Local fish supplier. Quality depends on the day and what came in. When it is good, it is genuinely good local catch. When it is thin, it is thin.

## St Barths

**Port type: Luxury top-up.** Quality is higher than most Caribbean islands. Prices are dramatically higher too.

### Quick Map

| Category | Order Online | Go In Person | Smart Tip |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **General** | — | **Chef's Market** | Carries items you cannot find elsewhere in the eastern Caribbean |

### The Cost Reality

A provision that costs EUR 8,000 in SXM costs EUR 12,000-14,000 here. Everything is imported and taxed.

> **Smart tip:** For a charter departing St Barths, provision as much as possible in SXM and ferry it across. SXM to St Barths is a 45-minute ferry. The savings on a full provision will exceed the freight cost several times over.

**Transport warning:** Sinclair Taxi — EUR 5,000 for a 20-minute ride on New Year's Eve. St Barths transport during peak season is extraordinary. Arrange marina-side delivery in advance or walk.

## Bahamas / Nassau

**Port type: Functional hub with a tax problem.** Better infrastructure than the eastern Caribbean, but approximately 40% import duty on everything defines the economics.

### Quick Map

| Category | Order Online | Go In Person | Smart Tip |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| **General** | **Sysco** (recurring orders) | **Fresh Market** | Sysco for volume recurring. Fresh Market for weekly. |
| **Fish** | — | **Milton Fresh Fish Market** | Local fish, generally good quality. Inspect on arrival. |
| **Meat** | **Sysco** / **BWA** | **Fresh Market** | Sysco and BWA for wholesale pricing. |
| **Dry Store** | **Sysco** / **BWA** | **Fresh Market** | Bulk ordering through Sysco saves vs retail + tax |
| **Frozen** | **Sysco** | — | Institutional but reliable for volume |
| **Agent** | **Dion Provisioning** (Exumas only) | — | Essential in the Exumas where options are near-zero |

### Category Detail

#### General / One-Stop

**Order online:**
- **Sysco** — Yes, Sysco operates in the Bahamas. Institutional format, reliable for volume, recurring order capability. For boats based here, build this relationship.
- **BWA** — Wholesale supplier. Useful for bulk orders at better-than-retail pricing.

**Go in person:**
- **Fresh Market** — Good produce and imported goods. Prices reflect the 40% import duty.

> **Smart tip:** If you are transiting from Florida, provision in Fort Lauderdale or Miami and bring everything on board. The savings on a full charter provision can exceed USD 5,000 vs buying the same items in Nassau with the 40% duty.

### Exumas

**Dion Provisioning** — The go-to and effectively the only option in the Exumas, where grocery infrastructure is near-zero. Contact well in advance — this is not a next-day operation.

> **Smart tip:** Plan Exumas provisioning as if there are no options. Bring everything. Be pleasantly surprised if Dion can fill gaps. Do not plan around Dion being able to source everything on your list.

### Price Reality

| Item | In Nassau (with 40% duty) | Pre-loaded from Miami | Saving |
|------|--------------------------|----------------------|--------|
| USDA Choice beef /kg | USD 65 | USD 35 @ Restaurant Depot | ~46% |
| Imported cheese /kg | USD 40 | USD 22 @ Costco | ~45% |
| Olive oil 5L | USD 55 | USD 30 @ Costco | ~45% |

**Bottom line:** The 40% import duty makes self-sourcing in Nassau expensive regardless. The real savings come from provisioning in the US before crossing.

### Category Detail

#### Ethnic & Specialty

**Go in person:**
- **Fruteria Mini Max** — Full Asian market. Lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime, rice noodles, Southeast Asian pantry staples, soy sauces, fish sauce, curry pastes, wonton wrappers. A rare find in Central America and the Caribbean.

> **Smart tip:** This is the best (and possibly only) proper Asian market between Miami and the Pacific coast of South America. Stock your entire Asian pantry here. Buy in bulk — what you do not buy now, you will not find at the next port.

#### General / One-Stop

**Go in person:**
- **RIBA Smith** — Multiple locations in Panama City. Imported goods, decent produce, reasonable prices. The day-to-day supermarket for guest and crew.
- **Merca Panama** — Wholesale market. Large, well-stocked. Produce, proteins, dairy in bulk. Worth a full morning to do the heavy lift here.

> **Smart tip:** RIBA Smith for the curated selection (guest-specific items, brands you recognise). Merca Panama for the volume and price. Do both on the same day — they cover different needs.

**Megayacht Provisions** — Full-service for yachts transiting the Canal or cruising the Pacific side. Expensive. Panama City has excellent self-sourcing, so the agent is only worth the markup when time is genuinely critical (Canal transit scheduling, tight turnaround).

### The Local Advantage

Costa Rica grows exceptional produce. Coffee, chocolate, tropical fruits, and vegetables from the Central Valley are outstanding. Lean into local product — it is better and cheaper than importing alternatives.

> **Smart tip:** Buy Costa Rican coffee at source (Tarrazú, Central Valley). Quality matches or exceeds what you pay EUR 30/kg for in Europe, at a third of the price. Buy 5-10kg and vacuum-seal. Guests love the story and the quality.

### If You Have No Time — The Agent

**Sea Masters** — Yacht provisioning logistics for the Pacific coast. Use them for coordination. Supplement with **Prime Provisions** for fresh local product.

## The Bottom Line

Caribbean provisioning demands a fundamentally different mindset than the Med. You do not resupply at every port. You plan three ports ahead and carry redundancy.

The chefs who thrive here do four things: load heavily at hub ports (Miami, San Juan, SXM, Panama), build relationships with the two or three key suppliers at each island, adapt menus to what is locally excellent rather than fighting to source European standards in a tropical environment, and carry enough dry stores and frozen backup to ride out a provisioning gap.

The Caribbean will never provision like the Cote d'Azur. But with the right loading strategy, the right contacts, and realistic expectations, you can feed guests at the same standard. You just have to think further ahead.
