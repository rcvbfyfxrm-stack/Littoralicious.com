# Know Your Rights: Sick Leave, MLC, and the DPA

*February 2026 · Global — All Flag States*

**The Development:**
Yacht chefs are getting sick at sea and being told to push through by captains who either do not know the law or choose to ignore it. The Maritime Labour Convention (MLC 2006, as amended) is unambiguous: if you fall ill or are injured during your employment, you are entitled to medical care and up to 16 weeks of paid sick leave at your basic wage. This is not discretionary. It is not contingent on the captain's opinion of your symptoms. It is international maritime law, ratified by every major flag state. The obligation falls on the shipowner from the first day of employment — any contract that imposes a 3-month waiting period before medical coverage begins is a red flag and potentially non-compliant.

**Who's Affected:**
Every yacht chef employed under a Seafarer Employment Agreement on an MLC-compliant vessel. This covers the vast majority of working superyachts registered under major flag states (Marshall Islands, Cayman Islands, Red Ensign Group, Malta, and others).

**What It Means:**
When a captain dismisses your illness, you have a legal escalation path. The Designated Person Ashore (DPA) is the management company's representative who exists specifically to handle safety and welfare concerns that cannot be resolved on board. The DPA is required under the ISM Code and their contact details must be posted aboard every vessel. If you are ill, the captain is unresponsive, and you need help, the DPA is your first call — not a request, not a favour, a right.

Real cases from the yacht chef community illustrate why this matters. One chef described being aboard at 0200 during rough seas, experiencing hypothermia-level body temperature (34.9C), low blood pressure, fatigue, dizziness, and migraines — with a captain who was "incredibly dismissive" and expected them to continue working. Another chef was put ashore at 0100 in Corsica after falling ill. In both cases, the chefs were entitled to medical attention, repatriation, and continued pay. Many did not know this.

**Key Details:**

| Detail | Specifics |
|--------|-----------|
| Sick leave entitlement | Up to 16 weeks at basic wage (MLC Standard A4.2) |
| Medical care | Shipowner covers all costs including hospitalisation, medication, and repatriation if required |
| Coverage start date | From day one of employment — no qualifying period |
| DPA contact | Must be posted aboard; contact the management company if not visible |
| ISWAN Helpline | +44 (0)300 012 4570 — free, confidential, 24/7 support for seafarers |
| Flag state complaint | Can be filed if the vessel is non-compliant; inspections can be triggered at any port |

**Your Move:**
Before your next embarkation, locate the DPA contact details aboard your vessel. Save the ISWAN helpline number in your phone: +44 (0)300 012 4570. If you are ill at sea and your captain tells you to keep working, put your request for medical assistance in writing — a text message or email to the captain with the DPA copied is a documented record. The MLC protects you. Use it.

**Source:** Maritime Labour Convention, 2006, as amended — Standard A4.2 (Shipowners' Liability). ISM Code, Section 4 — Designated Person Ashore. ISWAN (International Seafarers' Welfare and Assistance Network), iswan.org.uk. Yacht chef community case reports, 2025-2026.
