# Culinary Courses Worth Your Leave

*February 2026 · Global*

**What Happened:**
Yacht chefs are spending their leave on structured culinary education — not resort cooking classes, but intensive professional programmes that add measurable skills to a CV already built at sea. Data from two major yacht chef WhatsApp groups (1,300+ members combined) reveals a clear consensus on which courses deliver and which waste your time and money. The recommendations below come from chefs who attended these programmes, not from brochures.

**Who:**
Working yacht chefs with 2–15 years of galley experience, using rotation leave (typically 4–10 weeks) to acquire specific skills they cannot learn on board. The common gap: technique-specific training in disciplines where self-teaching hits a ceiling — sushi, pastry, breadmaking, regional cuisines.

**Why This Matters:**
A yacht chef's CV is built on versatility. The owner who wants omakase on Tuesday and Thai on Thursday does not care that you trained in French classical. Structured courses from credible institutions are the fastest way to add a genuine skill set — not a weekend introduction, but the kind of training that changes what you can put on a plate under pressure. The courses listed below were recommended by multiple working yacht chefs who attended them. Every listing includes duration, approximate cost, and the specific skill it adds.

**The Courses:**

### Sushi and Japanese

**Tokyo Sushi Academy — Tokyo, Japan**
Duration: 2 months (intensive) or 4+ weeks (shorter programme). Cost: approximately JPY 660,000 (EUR 4,100 / $4,400) for the 2-month course. Multiple yacht chefs have completed this programme, including Shauno (4+ weeks) and Rachel (recently graduated). Covers fish selection, knife work (yanagiba technique), rice preparation, nigiri shaping, and presentation. The 2-month programme includes a market visit component at Tsukiji outer market.

**Sushi School Tokyo — Tokyo, Japan**
Duration: 1–2 weeks. An alternative for chefs with shorter leave windows. Less depth than Tokyo Sushi Academy but covers fundamentals of rice preparation, knife cuts, and basic nigiri/maki.

### Thai

**Three Trees at Doi Saket — Chiang Mai Province, Thailand**
Duration: 1–4 weeks (modular). Described in the yacht chef community as "hands down the best school" for Thai cooking. Located outside Chiang Mai, it teaches northern Thai technique with a focus on mortar-and-pestle curry paste preparation, wok work, and regional dishes that do not appear on tourist menus. Small class sizes. Accommodation available on-site.

**SITCA (Siam International Thai Culinary Arts) — Ko Samui, Thailand**
Duration: 1–5 days (short courses) to 3 weeks (professional diploma). Located on Ko Samui, which makes it accessible during a Southeast Asia rotation stopover. Covers central and southern Thai cuisine.

**Blue Elephant — Bangkok and Phuket, Thailand**
Duration: Half-day to multi-day courses. The Bangkok location is more established. Known for structured Thai classical technique. The shorter formats make this suitable for a port call rather than a dedicated leave block.

### Bakery and Bread

**The Bertinet Kitchen — Bath, United Kingdom**
Duration: 1–5 day courses. Richard Bertinet's school focuses on hand-kneading technique, laminated doughs, sourdough, and French breadmaking. His approach to dough handling (wet doughs, no-knead methods) has specific application for yacht baking where proving conditions are variable and oven quality unreliable. Bath is a 90-minute train from London.

**Bakery Institute — Zaandam, Netherlands**
Duration: 5-day professional courses. Located near Amsterdam. Intensive programmes covering sourdough, viennoiserie, and advanced bread science. Geared toward working professionals, not hobbyists. Class sizes are small. Course language is English.

### Pastry

**Hoffman Barcelona — Barcelona, Spain**
Duration: 1–5 day masterclasses and longer professional programmes. Mey Hoffman's school is cited specifically for pastry by multiple yacht chefs. Barcelona's location makes it accessible during Med season, and the masterclass format fits a crew change window. Focus on chocolate work, entremets, and modern pastry technique.

### Yacht-Specific

**Galley Gang — Cornwall, United Kingdom**
Duration: 5 days. Cost: approximately GBP 1,500. Described as "fantastic" by multiple attendees. This is the only course on this list designed specifically for yacht chefs. Covers high-volume galley management, provisioning logistics, guest dietary management, and service under maritime conditions. Run by experienced yacht chefs. The Cornwall location doubles as a chance to visit the region's exceptional produce suppliers and fishing harbours.

### General Professional

**Ballymaloe Cookery School — Shanagarry, County Cork, Ireland**
Duration: 12-week certificate or shorter 1–5 day courses. Darina Allen's school has been producing professional cooks since 1983. The 12-week course covers breadmaking, butchery, fish cookery, gardening, and fermentation. The shorter courses are topic-specific. The farm-to-table philosophy and emphasis on whole-animal and whole-fish cookery translates directly to yacht provisioning, where waste is a cost and versatility is a requirement.

**Prue Leith Chef's Academy — Centurion, South Africa**
Duration: Various professional diplomas and short courses. Relevant for South African yacht chefs entering the industry. The professional diploma provides internationally recognised certification. Cost is significantly lower than equivalent European programmes — approximately ZAR 90,000 (EUR 4,500) for the full diploma.

### Online / Remote

**Plating Art — Online (Netherlands)**
Instructor: Michel van der Kroft. Format: video modules. Focused specifically on plate composition and visual technique. The online format means it can be completed on board during downtime — no leave required. Van der Kroft's work is rooted in Michelin-level service standards.

**The Detail Worth Knowing:**
The pattern across all recommendations is consistent: yacht chefs value depth over breadth, and they value programmes taught by practitioners over those taught by academics. The courses that get recommended repeatedly are the ones where the instructor still cooks professionally and the class sizes are small enough for individual correction. Every chef who mentioned Tokyo Sushi Academy or Three Trees at Doi Saket used the same word: intensive. That is the filter. If a programme does not push you harder than a busy charter week, it is a holiday, not an education.

**Source:** Yacht chef community recommendations, Chefs Helping Chefs and Yacht Chef Group WhatsApp, July 2025–January 2026. Individual programme details verified via institutional websites, February 2026.
