Japanese Ikejime Federation Updates Bleeding Protocol for Bluefin

The JIF has revised its recommended bleeding time for Pacific bluefin from 45 to 62 minutes based on ATP degradation studies. The extended window allows for more complete inosine monophosphate development—the compound responsible for peak umami in aged fish. Chefs should note this applies only to fish destined for 72+ hour aging.

Salt Crystallization Study Debunks "Finishing Salt Timing" Myth

New research from the Culinary Institute of Barcelona demonstrates that Maldon salt crystals dissolve within 47 seconds on protein surfaces above 55°C. The practice of "finishing just before service" to preserve texture has a window of approximately 30 seconds—not the 2-3 minutes commonly cited. For preserved crunch, application must occur tableside.
